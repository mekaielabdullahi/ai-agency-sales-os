## The 5 Questions

### **Q1: What is the biggest problem in your business right now?**

**Purpose**: Problem Discovery
**What you're learning**:
- Is there a real problem you can solve?
- Do they recognize it?
- How do they describe it? (Use their exact words later!)

**Why it matters**: Qualifies the lead and builds rapport through curiosity.

### **Q2: How much is this problem costing the business per month?**

**Purpose**: Cost Quantification & Pricing Anchor
**What you're learning**:
- What's the budget ceiling?
- How urgent is this? (Higher cost = more urgent)
- Do they understand the real cost?

**Why it matters**: **THIS IS YOUR PRICING FORMULA**
- If they say "$50K/month problem" â†’ You charge $15K-25K/month (30-50% of Q2)
- If they say "$10K/month problem" â†’ You charge $3K-5K/month

**If they say "I don't know":**

- "What's that in salaries?"

**Critical rule**: Don't move forward without a number. You can't price what you can't quantify.

### **Q3: How long have you had this problem for?**

**Purpose**: Timeline Assessment & Urgency
**What you're learning**:
- Is this new (low urgency) or chronic (high urgency)?
- Have they tried to fix it? What failed?
- How much have they already lost? (Q2 Ã— months = total pain)

### **Q4: If you do nothing, what does the next 6 to 12 months look like?**

**Purpose**: Consequence Amplification & Urgency Creation
**What you're learning**:
- Do they understand the stakes?
- Is there a consequence to waiting?
- What's their fear? (Use this in your close)

**Why it matters**: People buy to avoid pain more than to gain pleasure. Q4 paints the picture of pain if they don't act.

**The Consequence Ladder** (if their answer is weak):
- Them: "Things will stay the same."
- You: "And if that happens, then what?"
- Them: "We'll keep wasting time..."
- You: "And if that continues for 12 months, then what?"
- Them: "We'll fall behind competitors. Could affect funding."
- **Now you have real stakes.**

**This is where you create urgency without being pushy.**

### **Q5: If I had a magic wand and could build the perfect solution to fix this problem, what would that look like?**

**Purpose**: Solution Design & Proposal Blueprint
**What you're learning**:
- What do THEY actually want? (Not what you want to sell)
- What does success look like to them?
- What features/outcomes matter most?

**CRITICAL RULE**: Your proposal should use their EXACT words from Q5.

**Example**:
- Q5 answer: "I wish we had an AI system that pre-qualifies leads before they hit sales."

- **Use their language, not yours.**

**Why it matters**: By using their words from Q5, your proposal feels like you read their mind.

### **RULE #1: Questions Flow in Order**
Never skip ahead. Each builds on the previous.
- Q1 without Q2 = no pricing context
- Q2 without Q4 = no urgency
- Q5 without Q1-Q4 = solution without problem

### **RULE #2: Listen More Than You Talk**
Your job in Q1-Q5 is to EXTRACT information, not provide it.
- Ask the question â†’ Shut up â†’ Listen â†’ Clarify â†’ Move on
- Resist the urge to pitch until all 5 are answered

### **RULE #3: Their Answers ARE Your Proposal**
Copy/paste their language into your pitch.
- Use their Q1 problem description (verbatim)
- Anchor price to their Q2 cost
- Reference their Q3 timeline to show pain duration
- Use their Q4 consequence to create urgency
- Build your solution from their Q5 vision
