### Key Facts (From Transcript + Internal Discussion)
- **Target:** "Every swing-in you-know-what in town that has any decision-making power over land development staffing"
- **Decision-makers:** Land development PM, director, or VP of land
- **Market reality:** "This is a very small industry... you know every concrete vendor"
- **How they find contractors:** Ask a buddy, pick up the phone - NEVER Google
- **Established players:** Have "stable of maybe one, two, or at most three trades"
- **Breaking in:** "Takes personal relationships, previous experience"

### What Michael Said He Needs
> "I need to get out and get our marketing information in front of every swing-in you-know-what in town that has any decision-making power over land development staffing"

> "Phone attached to my ear all day, calling up people, taking people out to lunch, establishing those relationships, letting them know that we're here."

### UPDATE: Job Site Cold Outreach Failed (From Internal Discussion)

**Source:** Chris Andrade (tried it personally)

> "I did that and I went to 4 job sites, walked up, all legit. I had my gear, hard hat, boots, the whole nine... then they get pissed off... I go ask my other contractor buddies about how that - he's like, dude, if you walked up to me, random guy, yes, you're all geared up, but you hand me a flyer, I'm gonna tear that thing in front of your face and spit on it and laugh at you until you get the fuck off my job site."

**Implication:** Job site cold outreach is NOT viable. But other BD approaches need exploration.

### Alternative BD Approaches to Assess

**What HAS worked (from transcript):**
- Chris mentioned: "If I was at a bar or something or a restaurant and I just so happened to know somebody or see somebody, then that shit is really different"

**Potential approaches to research:**
1. **Industry watering holes** - Bars/restaurants where land developers hang out after work
2. **Association events** - Construction industry associations, builder groups
3. **Trade shows** - Less pressure than job sites
4. **Supplier networks** - Concrete suppliers know who's buying
5. **Referral incentives** - Existing contacts introducing new contacts
6. **LinkedIn relationship building** - Before in-person meeting

**Action:** Research how successful concrete subs break into new markets. Test 2-3 approaches.

**Technology Support:**
- Chatbot for scheduling/outreach with KNOWN contacts (Matt's idea)
- Relationship tracking for any approach that works
