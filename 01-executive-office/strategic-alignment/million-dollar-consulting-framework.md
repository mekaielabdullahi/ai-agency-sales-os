## The Alan Weiss Consulting Model

### 1. Value Upfront, Backend Revenue (The Real Weiss Model)

**CRITICAL INSIGHT:** Don't charge for audits/diagnostics - give value upfront for FREE.

**Why This Works Better:**
- Removes barrier to entry (clients don't know if it'll help yet)
- Builds trust through free value delivery
- Client sees the opportunity BEFORE being asked to pay
- Higher conversion rate (no price objection upfront)
- Backend revenue (implementation) is 10-30x larger than audit fees

**The Math:**
```
OLD MODEL (Paid Audit):
- Charge $500 for audit
- 50% of prospects say "too expensive" or "not sure"
- 5 audits = $2,500 revenue
- Maybe 1-2 convert to implementation ($10-15k)
- Total: $12,500-17,500

NEW MODEL (FREE Audit):
- Give audit for FREE
- 100% of prospects say yes (no cost barrier)
- 10 audits = $0 upfront revenue
- But 4-5 convert to implementation (better conversion)
- 5 implementations @ $15k avg = $75,000
- Total: $75,000 (4x-6x better!)
```

**Traditional Model (BAD):**
- "I'll code this for $X/hour"
- Ceiling on income (limited hours)
- Client watches the clock
- You're incentivized to work slowly

**Weiss Model (GOOD):**
- "Let me audit your business for FREE and show you the opportunities"
- Then: "This automation will save you $Y/year, my fee is $Z"
- Price implementation on client's ROI, not your time
- No clock-watching - focus on results
- Incentivized to deliver faster (more profit)

**Your Agency Application:**
```
Client Problem: Manual invoice processing (20 hrs/week)
Annual Cost: 20 hrs × 52 weeks × $30/hr = $31,200/year
Your Solution: Automation (saves 18 hrs/week)
Annual Value: 18 hrs × 52 weeks × $30 = $28,080/year saved

Value-Based Price: $8,000 (28% of first-year value)
```

### Pricing Psychology (Weiss)

**Rule 1: Never Present One Option**
- Always 3 options (good/better/best)
- Middle option is the target (80% choose this)
- High option makes middle look reasonable
- Low option anchors the value

**Rule 2: Anchor on Value, Not Cost**
```
❌ BAD: "This will cost $8,000"
✅ GOOD: "This saves you $28,000 per year.
          Your investment is $8,000 -
          you break even in 3.4 months."
```

**Rule 3: Bundle, Don't Itemize**
```
❌ BAD: "Design: $500, Dev: $3000, Testing: $500"
        (Client questions each line item)

✅ GOOD: "Complete automation solution: $8,000"
        (Client sees total value, not components)
```

**Rule 4: Options Structure**
```
Option 1: $5,000 (solves 70% of problem)
Option 2: $8,000 (solves 95% of problem) ⭐ RECOMMENDED
Option 3: $12,000 (solves 100% + extras)

Psychology:
- Option 1 feels incomplete
- Option 3 feels like overkill
- Option 2 is "just right" (Goldilocks)
```
