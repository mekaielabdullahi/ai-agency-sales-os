## THE 4 CONTENT PILLARS

### **PILLAR 1: Client Transformation Stories (40% of content)**

**Purpose:** Show real project outcomes, build credibility, demonstrate approach

**Format:** Narrative-driven case studies

**Structure Template:**
```
1. WHERE THEY STARTED
   - Specific pain point (20 hours/week wasted, $60K/month cost)
   - Manual processes, broken systems, scattered data

2. WHAT WE DISCOVERED (Discovery Phase)
   - The real problem (usually missing prerequisites)
   - What systems/processes were broken
   - Why past solutions failed

3. WHAT WE BUILT (Transformation)
   - Phase 1: Foundation (data integration, system connectivity, 4 weeks)
   - Phase 2: AI layer on solid foundation (4 weeks)
   - Phase 3: Training and adoption (2 weeks)

4. WHERE THEY ARE NOW (Outcomes)
   - Measurable results (time saved, cost reduced, efficiency gained)
   - ROI and timeline (6 months to break even)
   - Long-term impact (expanding to other departments)

5. WHAT I LEARNED (Founder Insight)
   - Key lesson from this project
   - What this reveals about AI implementation
   - Broader principle for audience
```

**Example Case Study Topics:**
- Defense contractor security reporting automation ($48K/month saved)
- Manufacturing material handling optimization (90% reduction in stockouts)
- E-commerce customer service automation (15 hours/week freed up)
- Construction project management system (recovered $500K+ lost revenue)

**Frequency:** 1-2 per week

**Content Deliverables:**
- LinkedIn post (800-1200 words)
- PDF carousel (8-10 slides with project breakdown)
- Short video option (Mekaiel walking through case study, 3-5 min)

### **PILLAR 2: Project Breakdowns / Workflow Demonstrations (30% of content)**

**Purpose:** Show transparency, demonstrate expertise, educate ICP

**Format:** Behind-the-scenes of how AriseGroup operates

**Topics:**
- "How we run discovery calls (the 5 Questions framework)"
- "Our prerequisites assessment checklist"
- "Week-by-week project timeline for AI implementation"
- "Why we turn down 40% of prospects (and how we decide)"
- "Tools and systems we use to deliver projects"

**Structure Template:**
```
1. HOOK
   - "Here's exactly how we..."
   - Promise transparency/behind-the-scenes

2. THE PROCESS (Step-by-step)
   - Numbered or bulleted breakdown
   - Specific details (not vague)
   - Why we do it this way (reasoning)

3. EXAMPLE OR DATA
   - Real scenario from recent project
   - Stat that proves it works (40% disqualified, 80% convert)

4. HOW YOU CAN USE THIS
   - Make it actionable for audience
   - "Ask yourself..." or "Look for..."

5. CTA
   - Offer full framework (comment for PDF)
   - Question for engagement
```

**Example Breakdown Topics:**
- The 5 Questions discovery framework (Q1-Q5 + internal Q6)
- Prerequisites checklist (Phase 0-5 readiness assessment)
- Our proposal structure (what we include, what we leave out)
- How we price projects (30-50% of Q2 cost + prerequisites)

**Frequency:** 1 per week

**Content Deliverables:**
- LinkedIn post (600-800 words)
- PDF carousel (6-8 slides explaining framework/process)
- Lead magnet (downloadable checklist/framework)

### **PILLAR 3: Founder Journey / Personal Learnings (20% of content)**

**Purpose:** Build trust through vulnerability, show values, humanize the brand

**Format:** Personal stories from building AriseGroup

**Topics:**
- Tough decisions ("I turned down $50K because...")
- Mistakes made ("The $50K mistake we almost made")
- Lessons from DoD/military ("9 years in DoD taught me...")
- Team dynamics ("How 4 founders collaborate without killing each other")
- Why we started AriseGroup ("The moment I realized vendors were lying")
- Values and principles ("Why integrity matters more than revenue")

**Structure Template:**
```

   - Specific scenario (turned down project, made tough call)

2. INTERNAL CONFLICT
   - What made this hard
   - Temptation to do the wrong thing
   - Stakes involved

3. WHAT I DID (AND WHY)
   - The choice made
   - Reasoning (values, long-term thinking)
   - Doubt or fear in the moment

4. WHAT HAPPENED
   - Outcome (sometimes good, sometimes painful)
   - Unexpected consequence

5. WHAT I LEARNED
   - Lesson for self
   - Broader principle for audience
   - Question: "Have you faced this?"
```

**Example Founder Story Topics:**
- "I turned down a $50K project because the client wasn't ready (and here's why that was hard)"

**Content Deliverables:**

### **PILLAR 4: Industry Insights / Market Observations (10% of content)**

**Purpose:** Position as industry observer, pattern recognition, thought leadership

**Format:** Analytical observations about AI transformation industry

**Topics:**
- "Why 80% of AI projects fail (pattern across 50+ projects I've analyzed)"

```

2. THE DATA/EXAMPLES
   - Specific pattern (Week 1-4, vendors do X, Week 5-8 this breaks)

3. THE CONSEQUENCE
   - What happens when this pattern plays out
   - Who gets hurt (usually the client)

   - How to avoid this
   - What to look for (red flags, green flags)

   - "If you're evaluating AI vendors, ask them..."
```

## CONTENT FORMATS (Per Shreya's Coaching)

### **1. PDF Carousels (HIGH PRIORITY)**

**Why PDFs:**
- LinkedIn algorithm favors PDFs (keeps users on platform = higher reach)
- Users can download/save (perceived value)
- Better mobile swipe experience than image carousels
- Easier to create (design in Canva, export as PDF, upload)

**How to Create:**
1. Design 8-10 slides in Canva (1080x1080px each)
2. Use templates above (Case Study, Framework, etc.)
3. Export as PDF
4. Upload to LinkedIn as "Document" (not image)
5. Write compelling post text to accompany

**Frequency Target:** 1 PDF carousel per week minimum

**Topics Best for PDF:**
- Case studies (8-10 slide breakdown)
- Frameworks (5 Questions, Prerequisites Checklist)
- Project timelines (Week-by-week what happens)
- Before/after transformations (multiple slides showing progression)

---

### **2. Video Content (MEDIUM PRIORITY)**

**Type 1: Loom Explainer Videos (3-5 minutes)**

**Best For:**
- Process walkthroughs ("Here's how we run discovery calls")
- Screen shares ("This is what broken data infrastructure looks like")
- Case study deep-dives (walk through project step-by-step)

**Format:**
- Screen recording with voiceover (Loom, Vidyard, or LinkedIn native)
- Talking head intro (10-15 sec: "Hey, Mekaiel here...")
- Visual walkthrough (2-4 min: screen + voiceover)
- Talking head outro + CTA (10-15 sec: "If this was helpful, follow for more")

**Production:**
- No fancy editing needed (authenticity > polish)
- Good lighting, clear audio (built-in mic is fine)
- Upload natively to LinkedIn (don't link to YouTube)

**Frequency:** 1 video every 2 weeks

---

**Type 2: Short-Form Videos (30-60 seconds)**

**Best For:**

**Format:**
- Talking head (phone camera, vertical or square)
- Fast-paced, energetic (hold attention)

- LinkedIn native upload

**Production:**
- Phone camera is fine (good lighting matters most)
- Subtitle with text overlays (80% watch without sound)
- 30-60 sec max (attention span)

**Frequency:** 1 short video per week (optional, test and see)
