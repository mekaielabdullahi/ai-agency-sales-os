## VOICE & TONE GUIDELINES

### **Mekaiel's Voice Characteristics:**

✅ **DO:**
- Write as founder/operator (first-person: "I", "we")
- Use specific numbers and data ($48K saved, 20 hours/week, 6 months ROI)
- Tell stories with beginning/middle/end (narrative arc)
- Be transparent about challenges and failures
- Give credit to team and clients (humble about wins)
- Use military/defense terminology when relevant (SIEM, DoD compliance, clearance)
- Show the "unsexy" work (data integration, process documentation)
- End with questions to audience (engage, don't lecture)

❌ **DON'T:**
- Hype or exaggeration ("revolutionary", "game-changing", "disrupting")
- Guru/teacher positioning ("I teach you", "here's what you need to know")
- Sales pitches ("Book a call", "Buy now", "Limited time")
- Generic platitudes ("Work hard", "Believe in yourself")
- Corporate jargon ("leverage synergies", "paradigm shift")
- Humble-bragging ("I'm so blessed", "grateful for this journey")
- Abstract concepts without concrete examples

### **Sentence Structure:**

**Good:**
- Short sentences. Clear thoughts. Easy to scan.
- Use line breaks generously (mobile-friendly)
- Bullets with → or ✓ for lists
- Section breaks with "---"
- One idea per paragraph

**Bad:**
- Long run-on sentences that try to pack multiple complex ideas into a single thought without giving the reader a chance to breathe or process the information being presented.
- Walls of text
- No visual structure

### **Emotion Level:**

**Authentic, not performative:**
- Share genuine frustration ("This shouldn't be this hard")
- Express real excitement ("I'm genuinely proud of this")
- Admit confusion ("I didn't see this coming")
- Show values ("This is why integrity matters")

**Not:**
- Over-the-top enthusiasm ("SO EXCITED!!!")
- Fake humility ("I'm nobody special")
- Forced vulnerability ("vulnerable share incoming...")

## SPECIAL INSTRUCTIONS

### **When Creating Case Studies (PILLAR 1):**

**Always include:**
1. Specific vertical (defense contractor, manufacturer, etc.)
2. Specific pain point (20 hours/week, $60K/month cost)
3. What was missing (prerequisites gap)
4. What we built (Phase 1 foundation, Phase 2 AI)
5. Measurable outcomes (time saved, cost reduced, ROI)
6. Timeline (10 weeks, 6 months later still expanding)
7. Founder lesson (what this taught me about AI implementation)

**Never:**
- Name the client (unless permission given)
- Share sensitive details (security protocols, classified info)
- Exaggerate results
- Imply AI was the main value (it's the foundation)

---

### **When Creating Founder Stories (PILLAR 3):**

**Always include:**
1. Personal decision or moment (turned down project, made tough call)
2. Internal conflict (felt bad, questioned myself)
3. Why I did it anyway (values, long-term thinking)
4. What I learned (lesson for audience)
5. Relatable question (have you faced this?)

**Never:**
- Humble-brag ("I'm so wise for turning down money")
- Criticize specific vendors or clients (stay professional)
- Make it about ego (make it about principles)

---

### **When Creating Project Breakdowns (PILLAR 2):**

**Always include:**
1. Transparency (here's how we actually do it)
2. Specific framework (5 questions, 3-phase process)
3. Why we do it this way (reasoning, not just what)
4. How audience can use it (make it actionable)
5. Offer to share more (download framework, comment for PDF)

**Never:**
- Gatekeep the knowledge ("buy my course to learn")
- Make it too complex (simplify for understanding)
- Skip the "why" (context matters)
