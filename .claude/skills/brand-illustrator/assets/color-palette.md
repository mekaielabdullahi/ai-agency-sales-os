# AriseGroup.ai Color Palette

## Overview

The Arise color palette balances trust and warmth. Deep navy anchors credibility while warm amber provides the guiding "light" that represents clarity and transformation.

---

## Primary Colors

### Warm Amber (The Guiding Light)
- **Hex**: `#E8914F`
- **RGB**: 232, 145, 79
- **Use**: Primary accent, highlights, energy, transformation moments
- **Emotion**: Warmth, guidance, clarity emerging

### Gold (Illumination)
- **Hex**: `#F5C26B`
- **RGB**: 245, 194, 107
- **Use**: Secondary accent, success indicators, premium touches
- **Emotion**: Achievement, value, optimism

### Soft Teal (Depth & Trust)
- **Hex**: `#2D7D8A`
- **RGB**: 45, 125, 138
- **Use**: Tertiary accent, contrast element, depth
- **Emotion**: Trust, stability, professionalism

### Deep Navy (Foundation)
- **Hex**: `#1A2B4A` (estimated - adjust as needed)
- **RGB**: 26, 43, 74
- **Use**: Text, outlines, grounding, structural elements
- **Emotion**: Authority, credibility, stability

## Neutral Colors

### Warm White
- **Hex**: `#FEFDFB`
- **RGB**: 254, 253, 251
- **Use**: Primary backgrounds, clean space
- **Note**: Never use pure white (#FFFFFF) - always warm it

### Soft Cream
- **Hex**: `#FDF8F3`
- **RGB**: 253, 248, 243
- **Use**: Secondary backgrounds, content areas
- **Note**: Creates depth against warm white

### Warm Gray (Light)
- **Hex**: `#E8E4DF`
- **RGB**: 232, 228, 223
- **Use**: Subtle backgrounds, borders, dividers

### Warm Gray (Medium)
- **Hex**: `#B8B2AA`
- **RGB**: 184, 178, 170
- **Use**: Secondary text, inactive elements, subtle details

### Warm Gray (Dark)
- **Hex**: `#5C574F`
- **RGB**: 92, 87, 79
- **Use**: Body text alternative, shadows base

## The Arise Glow (Signature Gradient)

### Full Gradient
```
Direction: Left to Right (or Bottom to Top for vertical applications)
Stop 1: Amber (#E8914F) at 0%
Stop 2: Gold (#F5C26B) at 50%
Stop 3: Soft Teal (#2D7D8A) at 100%
```

### Usage
- Represents the transformation journey
- The "arising" concept visualized
- Use as accent elements, backgrounds for key visuals, highlight treatments
- Hero moments and primary focal points

### Partial Gradients

**Warm Segment** (for subtle warmth)
```
Amber (#E8914F) → Gold (#F5C26B)
```
Use for: Light sources, energy, warmth accents

**Cool Segment** (for depth)
```
Gold (#F5C26B) → Soft Teal (#2D7D8A)
```
Use for: Depth, stability, grounding accents

## Color Ratios

### Recommended Distribution

| Color | Percentage | Application |
|-------|------------|-------------|
| Warm White/Cream | 50-60% | Backgrounds, breathing room |
| Deep Navy | 15-25% | Text, outlines, structure |
| Arise Glow (gradient) | 10-20% | Accents, highlights, focal points |
| Soft Teal | 5-10% | Depth, contrast, secondary accents |
| Warm Grays | 5-10% | Shadows, subtle details |

### The 60-30-10 Rule

- **60%**: Neutral backgrounds (warm white, cream)
- **30%**: Navy + warm grays (structure and text)
- **10%**: Arise Glow + accent colors (energy and focus)

## Color Combinations

### Primary Combination
- Background: Warm White (#FEFDFB)
- Primary Elements: Deep Navy (#1A2B4A)
- Accent: Arise Glow gradient
- Result: Clean, professional, warm

### Hero Combination
- Background: Soft Cream with subtle gradient wash
- Focal Element: Full Arise Glow gradient
- Supporting: Deep Navy outlines
- Result: Impactful, transformative, premium

### Subtle Combination
- Background: Warm White
- Primary: Warm Gray (Dark)
- Accent: Amber only (#E8914F)
- Result: Understated, sophisticated, clean

---

## Color Don'ts

### Never Use
- Pure white (#FFFFFF) - always warm it
- Pure black (#000000) - use Deep Navy instead
- Cold blues (like #0066CC or similar)
- Neon or oversaturated colors
- Cool grays (blue-tinted grays)

### Avoid
- More than 3 accent colors in one illustration
- Equal distribution of all colors (creates visual chaos)
- Accent colors in backgrounds (overwhelms)
- Navy as background (too heavy)

## Color Accessibility Notes

### Contrast Ratios
- Deep Navy on Warm White: Excellent readability
- Amber on Warm White: Use for accents only, not text
- Deep Navy on Cream: Good readability
- Teal on Warm White: Acceptable for larger elements

### For Text
- Primary text: Always Deep Navy on light backgrounds
- Never use Amber, Gold, or Teal for body text
- Accent colors for headings only if large enough
