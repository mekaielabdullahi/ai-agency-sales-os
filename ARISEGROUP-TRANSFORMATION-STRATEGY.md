### SHIFT #2: Define Your Monetization Strategy

**The 10X Insight:**
> "There's a spectrum from mercenary (pure cash) to missionary (co-own IP). The right model depends on opportunity size, client distribution, and your strategic goals."

**Current AriseGroup Model:**
- Pure cash-for-service (mercenary model)
- Pricing: 30-50% of Q2 monthly cost

**New Multi-Path Model:**

Create **three service tiers** based on opportunity assessment:

#### Tier 1: MERCENARY (Cash-for-Service)
**When to use:**
- Small-medium opportunity ($5K-15K/month projects)
- Client has no distribution beyond themselves
- You need cash flow
- Quick turnaround projects

**Structure:**
- Upfront payment or monthly retainer
- Client owns all IP
- Your current 5 Questions pricing (30-50% of Q2 cost)

**Example:** Local business needs AI phone agent for their own use

---

#### Tier 2: PARTNERSHIP (2-and-20 Model)
**When to use:**
- Medium-large opportunity ($15K-50K/month value)
- Measurable ROI metrics (cost savings, revenue increase)
- Client has some distribution capability
- You want upside participation

**Structure:**
- **Base fee:** 20-30% of Q2 cost (covers your operational costs)
- **Performance fee:** 20% of measurable savings/revenue for 12-24 months
- Shared success metrics

**Example:**
- Q2 Answer: "This costs us $40K/month in wasted time"
- Your pricing:
  - Base: $8K/month (20% of Q2)
  - Performance: 20% of actual savings achieved
  - If you save them $35K/month → You get $8K + $7K = $15K/month total
  - Their net savings: $20K/month
  - **Everyone wins**

**Action Items:**
1. Create performance tracking dashboard
2. Define measurable KPIs with client upfront
3. Monthly reporting on value delivered
4. Contract structure for performance fees

---

#### Tier 3: MISSIONARY (Co-Ownership/Equity)
**When to use:**
- Large opportunity ($50K+ monthly value or big TAM)
- Client has massive distribution (can scale beyond themselves)
- The solution could become standalone product
- You have capacity to build bigger

**Structure:**
- Reduced cash fee (or free)
- Equity stake in solution (30-50%)
- Co-develop and co-own IP
- Potential to spin out as standalone product

**Example:**
- Real estate company with 10,000 agents
- You build AI voice-to-contract system
- They want to offer it to every real estate agent in US
- You co-own the IP, scale together

**Decision Framework:**

After completing Q1-Q5, ask internally:

| Question | Mercenary | Partnership | Missionary |
|----------|-----------|-------------|------------|
| **Opportunity size** | <$15K/month | $15K-50K/month | $50K+/month or large TAM |
| **Client distribution** | Self-use only | Some scalability | Massive network |
| **Measurable ROI** | Hard to track | Clear metrics | Transformational |
| **Your capacity** | Limited | Medium | Full commitment |
| **Cash flow needs** | High | Medium | Low |

**Action Items:**
1. Create internal assessment scorecard
2. Train sales team on when to propose each model
3. Develop contract templates for each tier
4. Create case studies for each monetization type

### SHIFT #4: CTO → CPO Positioning

**The 10X Insight:**
> "Don't be an order-taker (CTO). Be a strategic partner (CPO). You should be helping clients define what to build, not just executing their ideas."

**Current AriseGroup Approach:**
- Q5 asks: "What's your magic wand solution?"
- You build what they describe

**Enhanced Approach:**

**After Q5, add this:**

"That's interesting. Based on what you described and working with [similar companies], here's what I'd actually recommend..."

**Then you prescribe:**
1. Their Q5 vision (acknowledge it)
2. **Your strategic POV:**
   - "But before we build that, here's what you actually need..."
   - Introduce prerequisites
   - Suggest better architecture
   - Recommend phased approach

**Example:**

**Client Q5:** "I want an AI that answers all our customer questions"

**Old approach:** "Great, we'll build that. $15K/month."

**New approach:**
"That makes sense, and we can build that. But based on what you told me about your current systems, here's what I'd actually recommend:

**Phase 1 (Prerequisites - Month 1-2):**
- Build centralized knowledge base (you don't have one)
- Integrate your 3 separate customer data systems
- Document your top 50 customer questions

**Phase 2 (AI Layer - Month 2-3):**
- Deploy AI assistant trained on your knowledge base
- Connect to unified customer data
- Handle tier-1 support automatically

**Why this order:** If we build the AI first without the foundation, it'll give wrong answers because your data is scattered. We'd be building on sand.

**Investment:**
- Phase 1: $12K (prerequisites)
- Phase 2: $8K/month ongoing (AI system + maintenance)

**Timeline:** Live in 10 weeks instead of 4, but actually works.

Make sense?"

**This is CPO positioning.**

**Action Items:**
1. Train team on "prescribe, don't just execute"
2. Create prerequisites assessment checklist
3. Develop phased proposal template
4. Position as "strategic partner, not dev shop"

#### Component 2: Ongoing Retainer (Recurring)
- **Tier 1: Maintenance & Support** ($2K-5K/month)

  - Updates
  - Training
  - Support tickets

- **Tier 2: Engineering-as-a-Service** ($5K-15K/month)
  - Ongoing feature development
  - Process optimization
  - System expansion
  - Regular improvements

- **Tier 3: AI Operating System** ($15K-30K/month)
  - Full stack management
  - Continuous improvement
  - Strategic consulting
  - Priority support

**Sales Process Update:**

After closing initial project, immediately pitch retention:

"This initial build gets you to [Q5 outcome]. But here's what smart companies do:

They keep us on retainer at $[X]/month for [duration] because:

Most of our clients start with the initial build, love it, then realize they need ongoing support.

I recommend locking in [Tier 2: Engineering-as-a-Service] now so you're not starting from scratch in 3 months when you want more features.

Make sense?"

**The Progression:**

**This is the 10X model.**
