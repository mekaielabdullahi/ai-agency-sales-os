# Discovery Call Methodology

**Source**: Client Outreach Skill + Business Functions Mapping
**Purpose**: Systematic approach to 30-minute AI Readiness Audit calls

---

## Overview

The discovery call is where you provide immediate value while gathering information to create a detailed audit or proposal.

**Goal**: Client leaves with insights, you leave with roadmap for how to help them.

---

## Pre-Call Preparation (15 min before call)

**Research Checklist**:
- [ ] Review LinkedIn profile thoroughly
- [ ] Check company website
- [ ] Review recent posts/activity
- [ ] Identify 2-3 likely pain points based on industry
- [ ] Prepare specific questions based on their business

**Materials Ready**:
- [ ] Note template open
- [ ] Business functions mapping framework ready
- [ ] AI opportunity checklist available
- [ ] Calendar ready for next steps scheduling

---

## Call Structure (30 minutes)

### Minutes 0-5: Rapport & Context

**Opening**:
"Thanks for making time, [Name]. I have you for 30 minutes to explore AI opportunities in [Company]'s operations. Sound good?"

**Context Setting**:
"What prompted your interest in systematic AI adoption?"

**Listen For**:
- Their biggest current challenge
- What they've already tried
- What they think AI might help with

---

### Minutes 5-20: Discovery Questions

**Current State Questions**:
1. "Walk me through a typical day/week. Where does your time go?"
2. "What are your biggest operational friction points right now?"
3. "What manual processes eat the most time?"
4. "Where do bottlenecks happen in your business?"

**AI Context Questions**:
5. "Have you explored AI tools? What worked or didn't?"
6. "What's blocking systematic AI adoption right now?"
7. "If you could automate one thing, what would have biggest impact?"

**Strategic Questions**:
8. "What's your main business goal for next 6-12 months?"
9. "How do operations need to change to support that goal?"
10. "What does success look like for AI adoption?"

**Business Functions Questions**:
11. "What are the major areas of work in your business?" (Map functions)
12. "Which functions feel most inefficient or problematic?"

---

### Minutes 20-28: Value Mapping

**Share 2-3 Specific Opportunities**:
"Based on what you shared, I see [#] clear opportunities..."

**Structure Your Response**:
1. **Quick Win** (30 days):
   - "In [specific function], you could [specific automation]"
   - "Impact: [time/cost saving estimate]"
   - "This would be your fastest ROI"

2. **Strategic Initiative** (90 days):
   - "For [their stated goal], [specific AI system]"
   - "Impact: [business outcome]"
   - "Requires more setup but high value"

3. **(Optional) Long-term** (6 months):
   - "Eventually, [transformational change]"
   - "This is where you'd see [major impact]"

**Make It Specific**:
- ❌ "You could use AI for automation"
- ✅ "Your client onboarding could use AI to auto-generate project folders, send welcome emails, and populate CRM—saving 2 hours per client"

---

### Minutes 28-30: Next Steps

**Hot Prospect** (very interested):
"I'd like to create a detailed AI Readiness Audit for [Company]. I'll map your entire operation and create a phased roadmap. Takes me 2-3 hours, I'll send it by [date]. Worth doing?"

**Warm Prospect** (interested, needs time):
"I'll send you a summary of what we discussed plus resources on [their specific challenge]. Can we reconnect in 2 weeks to discuss next steps?"

**Not Ready**:
"This was helpful. Mind if I follow up in [timeframe]? I'll send some resources in the meantime."

**Always Schedule Next Step Before Hanging Up**

## Listening Signals

**Hot Prospect Signals**:
- Asks detailed questions about implementation
- Mentions budget or timeline
- Wants to schedule follow-up immediately
- Asks "what would this cost?"
- Mentions decision-making process

**Warm Prospect Signals**:
- Interested but needs to think
- Wants to discuss with team
- Asks for more information
- Engaged but cautious

**Cold Prospect Signals**:
- Just exploring generally
- No specific pain points
- Can't articulate what success looks like
- Vague timeline
- Low engagement

---

## Question Techniques

**Go Deeper**:
- "Tell me more about that"
- "What happens when [pain point] occurs?"
- "How much time does [process] take?"
- "What's the business impact of [challenge]?"

**Quantify**:
- "How often does this happen?"
- "How many hours per week?"
- "What does this cost you?"
- "How many people are affected?"

**Prioritize**:
- "If you could fix one thing, what would it be?"
- "What's the most urgent challenge?"
- "What's preventing you from [goal]?"

---

## Common Mistakes

**❌ Talking Too Much**
- You should talk 30%, they should talk 70%
- Listen more than you pitch

**❌ Pitching Instead of Discovering**
- Don't pitch services
- Ask questions, provide value

**❌ Not Taking Notes**
- Memory fails
- Notes create better audit/proposal

**❌ Going Over Time**
- Respect their 30 min
- Set timer
- End on time (builds trust)

**❌ Vague Next Steps**
- Always schedule specific follow-up
- Get calendar invite out before call ends

## Post-Call Actions (30 min within 4 hours)

**Immediate**:
1. Send thank-you email
2. Send calendar invite for next step
3. Clean up notes
4. Add to CRM/tracking

**Within 24 Hours**:
1. Send promised resources
2. Begin audit creation (if committed)

**Thank You Email Template**:
```

→ [Pain point 1]
→ [Pain point 2]
→ [Opportunity you identified]

I'll send over the detailed AI Readiness Audit by [date].
It'll include specific recommendations for [their main challenge].

Any questions in the meantime, just reach out.

Looking forward to next steps!

Best,
[Your Name]
```

## Success Metrics

**Good Discovery Call**:
- Clear understanding of their business
- 3-5 specific pain points identified
- 2-3 opportunities mapped
- Next step scheduled
- Client felt heard and got value

**Poor Discovery Call**:
- Mostly you talking
- Vague pain points
- No clear opportunities
- No next step scheduled
- Client confused about value
