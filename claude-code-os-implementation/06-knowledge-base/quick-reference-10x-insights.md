# Quick Reference: 10X AI Transformation Insights

**Source:** Alex Lieberman & Arman (10X co-founders) + Liam & Nick (Morningside AI)
**Date:** December 2, 2025

---

## The Big Picture in 60 Seconds

- AI transformation is a **10+ year arbitrage** (limited by humans, not technology)
- "AI" is often a **Trojan horse** - 70-80% of work is traditional software/prerequisites
- **Multiple monetization paths** exist (mercenary → 2-and-20 → missionary → full ownership)
- **Specialization wins** for 99% of agencies (unless you have built-in distribution)
- **Training/change management** is the most defensible moat
- Industry will **10x to ~10,000 firms** in next 12 months

---

## Three Founding Criteria (10X Model)

1. **Control & Freedom** → No VC pressure, maintain autonomy
2. **Attract Top Talent** → Former founders through unique comp model
3. **Recurring Revenue** → Engineering-as-a-service, not just project work

**Why consulting first:** Cash flow from day one + maximum optionality

## The "Prerequisites" Reality

Every AI project has 3 layers:

| Layer | % of Work | What It Is |
|-------|-----------|------------|
| **Prerequisites** | 70% | Data infrastructure, process docs, integrations, basic software |
| **Traditional Software** | 20% | Custom tools, workflows, automation |
| **Actual Gen AI** | 10% | "Literally one API call" (but needs 2 months of prep) |

**Client Pattern:**
- 80% of executive wish lists = traditional software
- 20% = actual Gen AI needs

**Your Response:** Make prerequisites a feature, not a bug. This IS the value.

## Monetization Spectrum Quick Guide

| Model | When to Use | Structure | Risk/Reward |
|-------|-------------|-----------|-------------|
| **Mercenary** | Small opportunity, need cash flow | Pure cash-for-service | Low/Low |
| **2-and-20** | Mid-size, measurable ROI | 2% mgmt fee + 20% upside | Med/Med |
| **Missionary** | Large opportunity, strong distribution | Co-own equity/IP | High/High |
| **Venture** | Biggest belief in opportunity | Full ownership | Highest/Highest |

**Decision Factors:**
- ✓ Opportunity size
- ✓ Client distribution capability
- ✓ Can they scale beyond themselves?
- ✓ Your cash flow needs
- ✓ Strategic goals (cash vs IP building)

## The Human Bottleneck

**Why 10+ Years of Runway:**

> "Not because technology takes 10 years to mature, but because humans will stay in the way of effectively replacing ourselves."

**Evidence:** Companies still on on-premise servers in 2025 (cloud is 15+ years old)

**Implication:** Don't worry about saturation. Focus on change management.

**Most Limiting Factor:** Human creativity, not technical capability

## Specialist vs Generalist

### Go Specialist If:
- ❌ You don't have built-in audience/distribution
- ✅ You want software partner relationships (Snowflake, AWS, etc.)
- ✅ You want efficient referral engine
- ✅ You want "no one got fired for buying IBM" positioning

### Stay Generalist If:
- ✅ You have large personal brand (Alex's audience)
- ✅ Built-in distribution channel
- ✅ Trust established outside vertical expertise

### The Path to Specialization:
1. Host dinners with C-suite in target vertical
2. Win first customer, over-deliver
3. Get 2-3 referrals in same vertical
4. Approach software partners as "[vertical] specialist"
5. Fill their partnership gap

## Digital Franchise Model

**Concept:** General brand with vertical-specific subsidiaries

**Options:**
1. **Same team, different landing pages:** "10X Industrial," "10X Retail," etc.
2. **Dedicated pods:** Engineers organized by vertical
3. **Actual franchises:** Independent operators, licensed methodology

**Benefit:** Capture specialist positioning while maintaining centralized excellence

## CTO vs CPO Positioning

**Don't Be Order-Taker (CTO):**
- Client gives PRD
- You just execute
- Commodity pricing

**Be Strategic Partner (CPO):**
- Help define product direction
- Create PRD together
- Premium positioning
- Share perspective on stack/approach

**Why:** Most of your contacts aren't CTOs. CEOs/COOs/CFOs need product guidance, not just execution.

**The Naming Power:**
> "If we didn't call ourselves an AI transformation firm, we wouldn't be able to attract the talent we have. If we called ourselves 'world's best dev shop,' I guarantee we wouldn't have these engineers."

**Target:** Former founders, not just engineers

- Entrepreneurial mindset

## Technology Assets Strategy

**Question:** To what extent build tech as business asset?

**Palantir Model:** Technology underpinning all client work

**Approach:**
1. What do you rebuild for every client?
2. How can you create reusable building blocks?
3. What IP improves your margins?

**McKinsey Pattern:** Acquiring SaaS companies to leave behind software with clients

**Lesson:** Smartest consultancies becoming software companies

## The 10+ Year Vision

**Stage 1 (Now):** Services business generating cash flow

**Stage 2 (1-3 years):** Services + technology assets + partnerships

**Stage 3 (3-5 years):** Services + products + IP ownership

**Stage 4 (5-10 years):** "Research lab" funded by cash flows

**End State:** Center of innovation with:
- Brilliant people
- Forcing function (real client problems)
- Freedom (financial + creative)

**Model:** Google X, but self-funded through services vs VC capital
