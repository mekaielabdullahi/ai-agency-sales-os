## Topics

### Alyssa's Role & Daily Workflow

- Alyssa is the operational center, managing all incoming communication and Jobber updates.
- Her day (8:30-9:00 AM start) involves:
  - Reviewing emails & texts for new jobs.
  - Invoicing completed jobs from the prior day.
  - Scheduling new service calls & deliveries.
  - Managing the parts ordering workflow (quotes, POs, tracking).
  - Preparing parts for technicians using a dedicated shelf system.

### Communication & Scheduling Bottlenecks

- The primary workflow disruption is Kelsey's communication style, which forces manual work on Alyssa.
- **Communication Channels:**
  - **Kelsey's Personal Phone:** Top channel for customer orders and service requests, bypassing the office.
  - **Email:** Alyssa manages ~10 inboxes, which helps her prioritize but is inefficient.
  - **Jobber Text:** Used for post-scheduling updates (e.g., "on my way"); Kelsey likes it because it keeps business texts off his personal phone.
- **Scheduling Process:**
  - Kelsey prefers 3-4 service calls/day; >5 causes stress and long hours.
  - Alyssa must get Kelsey's approval for all new service appointments to avoid conflicts with his unstated priorities.
  - Kelsey will call Alyssa to move jobs on the Jobber calendar instead of using the app himself.

- **Parts Ordering Workflow:**

  2. Alyssa gets vendor quote (24-48 hr turnaround).
  3. Alyssa sends customer quote, requiring upfront payment for parts.
  4. Upon payment, Alyssa places PO and tracks shipment.

- **RMA Process Failures:**
  - Ink vendors (e.g., STS Inks) deny RMAs for stock held >30 days, citing their return policy.
  - This forces Kelsey to intervene directly with vendor owners.
  - Result: Defective product sits on the shelf for months, unresolved.

### Order Fulfillment & Print Services

- **Fulfillment Channels:**
  - **Local Delivery:** For Phoenix-area customers, Joe delivers on his service route.
  - **Shipping:** For customers outside the Phoenix area (e.g., Sholo), orders are shipped.
  - **Drop Shipping:** Used for non-local orders or when office stock is low.
- **Shopify Portal Ineffectiveness:**
  - Customers rarely use the portal.
  - When they do, they often order the wrong ink, requiring Alyssa to call and correct the order.
- **Unprofitable Print Services:**
  - Print services are not profitable, often breaking even or losing money.
  - Purpose: To keep machines running, as inactivity causes breakdowns and ink drying.
  - Pricing is significantly lower than professional print shops.
