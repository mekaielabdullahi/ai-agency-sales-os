# Daily Operating Cost (DOC) Assessment Framework

## Purpose
Gauge client's financial awareness. "Capital is the bloodstream of a business" - understand where their money flows.

---

## Two Question Versions

### Version 1: Direct (For financially savvy clients)
> "What does it cost you to run AZ Inflatable Events every single day - just to keep the doors open?"

### Version 2: Breakdown (For clients who don't track daily costs)
> "Let's break this down together. If you think about what you spend each day on labor, marketing, equipment maintenance, and operations - what would you estimate that total is?"

---

## DOC Categories to Explore

### 1. Labor & Human Capital
- Crew wages (setup, delivery, operations)
- Training time
- Administrative staff
- Management overhead

**AI Opportunity:** Automated scheduling, training videos, coordination apps

### 2. Marketing & Customer Acquisition
- Digital ads
- Website maintenance
- Social media management
- Referral costs

**AI Opportunity:** Automated lead follow-up, chatbots, content generation

### 3. Equipment & Inventory
- Maintenance
- Storage
- Insurance
- Depreciation

**AI Opportunity:** Predictive maintenance, inventory tracking, QR-based equipment logs

### 4. Operations & Logistics
- Fuel/delivery
- Route planning
- Setup time
- Communication overhead

**AI Opportunity:** Route optimization, digital setup guides, crew tracking

### 5. Safety & Compliance
- Inspections
- Training
- Documentation
- Insurance

**AI Opportunity:** Digital checklists, QR emergency systems, automated compliance

### 6. Opportunity Costs (Hidden Bleed)
- Lost bookings (slow response)
- Underpriced jobs
- Inefficient crew time
- Customer churn

**AI Opportunity:** This is where biggest ROI lives

## When to Use DOC in Meeting

**Option 1:** After Q2 (while talking money)
> "You mentioned [Q2 cost]. Let me ask - do you know what it costs to run this business every single day?"

**Option 2:** After Q5 (zoom out to total impact)
> "Before I show you what this could look like, help me understand the bigger picture. What's your daily operating cost?"

## Sample Script for AZ Inflatable

**If they know their numbers:**
> "You run a $3M operation. That's roughly $8,000 a day just to exist. Where do you feel like that $8,000 isn't working as hard as it could?"

**If they don't know:**
> "Let's rough this out. How many crew members on a typical day? What's the average wage? OK so labor alone is about $X per day..."

---

## Why This Matters for the $10K Assessment

The DOC conversation:
1. Reveals their financial sophistication
2. Establishes pricing anchor (if daily cost is $8K, $10K assessment is ~1 day of operations)
3. Shows you understand business, not just tech
4. Identifies where biggest ROI opportunities are
5. Their numbers become the ROI justification in your proposal
