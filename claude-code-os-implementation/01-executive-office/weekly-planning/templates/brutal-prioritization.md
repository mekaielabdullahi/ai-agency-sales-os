# Brutal Prioritization Framework

**Purpose**: Ruthlessly eliminate everything except what truly matters.

---

## üéØ Core Philosophy

**The Rule**: If it's not moving you toward your OBG, it's optional at best, wasteful at worst.

**The Question**: "If I could only do ONE thing this week, what would it be?"

---

## üìã The Brutal Questions

### Question 1: The Necessity Test
**For each item on your list, ask**:
> "What happens if I don't do this at all?"

- **Nothing bad happens** ‚Üí ELIMINATE
- **Minor inconvenience** ‚Üí DEFER
- **Significant setback** ‚Üí EVALUATE FURTHER
- **Critical failure** ‚Üí KEEP (maybe)

---

### Question 2: The OBG Alignment Test
**For remaining items**:
> "Does this directly advance my One Big Goal?"

| Response | Action |
|----------|--------|
| Yes, directly | ‚úÖ KEEP - High Priority |
| Yes, indirectly | ‚ö†Ô∏è EVALUATE - Medium Priority |
| Not really | ‚ùå DEFER or DELEGATE |
| No | ‚ùå ELIMINATE |

**OBG Scoring**:
- 9-10: Critical path to OBG
- 7-8: Supports OBG significantly
- 4-6: Tangentially related
- 1-3: No real connection
- 0: Actively distracts from OBG

---

### Question 3: The Leverage Test
**For high-priority items**:
> "Will this make everything else easier or unnecessary?"

**High Leverage** = Do this FIRST
**Medium Leverage** = Schedule strategically
**Low Leverage** = Question if needed

---

### Question 4: The Energy ROI Test
> "Is the energy required worth the outcome?"

```
Value Generated / Energy Required = ROI
```

- **High ROI** (>3:1): Do it
- **Medium ROI** (1:1 to 3:1): Schedule carefully
- **Low ROI** (<1:1): Eliminate or delegate

---

### Question 5: The Constraint Test
> "Is this THE constraint blocking everything else?"

**Theory of Constraints**: Only ONE thing is the true bottleneck at any time.

- **Is this the constraint?** YES ‚Üí Top priority
- **Is this the constraint?** NO ‚Üí Deprioritize


## üö´ The Kill List Rules

### Auto-Kill Categories
These should almost always be eliminated:

1. **Fake Urgency**
   - Appears urgent but isn't important
   - Example: Most emails, meetings, "quick questions"

2. **Busy Work**
   - Makes you feel productive without real output
   - Example: Excessive planning, reorganizing, "research"

3. **Premature Optimization**
   - Perfecting something that doesn't need to exist yet
   - Example: Polish before validation

4. **Scope Creep**
   - "Nice to haves" disguised as requirements
   - Example: Extra features, additional reports

5. **Other People's Priorities**
   - Important to them, not to your OBG
   - Example: Favors, networking for networking's sake

6. **Low-Leverage Learning**
   - Learning without application
   - Example: Courses, books not tied to current OBG

---

## ‚úÖ The Keep List Rules

### Auto-Keep Categories
These usually make the cut:

1. **Constraint Breakers**
   - Directly removes your main bottleneck

2. **OBG Critical Path**
   - Can't reach OBG without it

3. **High-Leverage Actions**
   - One action, multiple benefits

4. **Revenue Generators** (if revenue is OBG-aligned)
   - Directly creates money/progress

5. **System Builders**
   - Creates automation/leverage for future
