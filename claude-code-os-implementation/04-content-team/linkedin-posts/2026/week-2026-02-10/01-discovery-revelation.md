## POST (Copy-paste ready):

We thought we were solving a phone problem.

We were wrong.

Phase 1 for our client: Build an AI phone system to capture calls and auto-create service requests.

It worked. Calls flowing into their CRM. AI summaries. No more screenshot bombing between field and office.

Done. Next problem.

Except...

THE REAL DISCOVERY:

When we interviewed the operations manager for Phase 2 planning, she said something that stopped the conversation:

"Maybe about four or five hours a day on email."

50% of her week.

10 inboxes.

And we'd been focused on phones.

HERE'S WHAT I LEARNED:

The first discovery call reveals the problem they know about.

The fourth interview reveals the problem that's actually eating their margin.

Phase 1 fixed the squeaky wheel (phones, interruptions, handoff chaos).

Phase 2 now targets the silent killer: 20-25 hours/week buried in email management.

THE NUMBERS:
- Phase 1: $5K investment, $70K+ annual value created
- Phase 2: $10-15K investment, $67-83K annual value
- Combined: 30-40 hours/week recovered for the business

THE LESSON:

Your client's first problem isn't their biggest problem.

It's the one they have language for.

The real opportunity shows up when you interview the people doing the workâ€”not just the owner signing the check.
