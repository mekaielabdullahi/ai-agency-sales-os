## The Hook Formula Library

### Formula 1: Curiosity Gap
**Pattern**: "Most people think X, but actually Y..."

**Examples**:
- "Most businesses waste 30+ minutes daily on planning. Here's the 60-second alternative."
- "Everyone says wake up at 5 AM. I did the opposite and 3x'd my output."
- "The #1 productivity tip is actually killing your productivity."

**When to Use**: Educational content, contrarian takes, surprising insights
**Strength**: High curiosity, demands resolution

---

### Formula 2: Big Promise
**Pattern**: "In the next [timeframe], you'll learn/get/discover [specific benefit]"

**Examples**:
- "In 3 minutes, you'll know exactly where your business operations are breaking down."
- "This one system will save you 10+ hours weekly."
- "The framework that took my productivity from 5/10 to 8/10 consistently."

**When to Use**: Educational content, tutorials, value-heavy posts
**Strength**: Clear value, specific benefit

---

### Formula 3: Pattern Interrupt
**Pattern**: Strong, unexpected statement that breaks scroll

**Examples**:
- "Stop trying to be productive."
- "Your morning routine is wrong."
- "I wasted $40K learning this lesson."
- "Delete your task manager."

**When to Use**: Contrarian content, strong opinions, attention grabbing
**Strength**: Maximum interruption, high curiosity

---

### Formula 4: Authority Statement
**Pattern**: "[Years/Experience] taught me [surprising insight]"

**Examples**:
- "20 years in operations taught me: systems beat motivation every time."
- "After building secure systems for embassies, I realized organizations need the same discipline for AI adoption."
- "10,000 hours of project management condensed into 3 principles."

**When to Use**: Establishing credibility, experience-based insights
**Strength**: Builds trust, signals value

---

### Formula 5: Problem Agitation
**Pattern**: "You're losing [specific thing] because [specific reason]"

**Examples**:
- "You're losing 2 hours daily to poor planning. Here's why."
- "Your business is leaking revenue through inefficient operations."
- "Every morning you delay planning costs you 30 minutes of peak energy."

**When to Use**: Promotional content, problem-aware audiences
**Strength**: Creates urgency, motivates action

---

### Formula 6: Contrarian View
**Pattern**: "Everyone's wrong about X. Here's why..."

**Examples**:
- "Everyone's wrong about productivity. It's not about time management."
- "The advice to 'work harder' is killing organizations."
- "Hustle culture is a scam. Here's what actually works."

**When to Use**: Thought leadership, differentiation, bold positioning
**Strength**: Stands out, sparks debate

---

### Formula 7: Transformation Story
**Pattern**: "[Time] ago I was [before]. Now I'm [after]."

**Examples**:
- "6 months ago I spent 30 minutes planning daily. Now: 60 seconds."
- "I went from working 60-hour weeks to 40-hour weeks. Revenue up 40%."
- "Last year: drowning in operations. This year: everything systematized."

**When to Use**: Personal stories, case studies, proof
**Strength**: Relatable, demonstrates results

---

### Formula 8: Question Hook
**Pattern**: "[Provocative question]"

**Examples**:
- "Is your business running you, or are you running your business?"
- "Know exactly where your time goes each day?"
- "Working hard but not winning?"
- "What if the problem isn't effort, but systems?"

**When to Use**: Engagement posts, thought-provoking content
**Strength**: Invites participation, sparks self-reflection

---

### Formula 9: Specific Number/Data
**Pattern**: "[Surprising statistic] + [what it means]"

**Examples**:
- "73% of business owners work 60+ hours but can't say where the time goes."
- "The average business owner spends 12.5 hours weekly on tasks they could automate."
- "$40K wasted on a system I never used. Here's what I learned."

**When to Use**: Data-driven content, authority building
**Strength**: Credibility, concrete proof

---

### Formula 10: Direct Benefit
**Pattern**: "[Specific outcome] without [common pain]"

**Examples**:
- "Double your output without working more hours."
- "Systematic operations without the complexity."
- "Plan your day in 60 seconds, not 30 minutes."

**When to Use**: Value propositions, promotional content
**Strength**: Clear promise, pain relief

## Platform-Specific Hook Adaptations

### LinkedIn Hooks
- **Length**: 1-2 sentences (max 3)
- **Tone**: Professional yet personable
- **Style**: Can be longer, more context
- **Best**: Problems, insights, stories, questions

**Example**:
"I used to spend 30+ minutes every morning just figuring out what to work on.
By the time I had priorities straight, my peak energy was gone."

### Twitter Hooks
- **Length**: 200-240 characters
- **Tone**: Punchy, direct
- **Style**: One quick punch
- **Best**: Controversial, surprising, direct

**Example**:
"Stop trying to be more productive.

Start building better systems."

### Email Subject Lines
- **Length**: 40-50 characters
- **Tone**: Curiosity or benefit-driven
- **Style**: Tight, specific
- **Best**: Questions, numbers, curiosity gaps

**Examples**:
- "The 60-second planning system"
- "Why your productivity system fails"
- "3 systems that save 10 hours weekly"
- "Is your business running you?"

### Blog Headlines
- **Length**: 60-70 characters (SEO optimal)
- **Tone**: Clear value proposition
- **Style**: Keyword-inclusive
- **Best**: How-to, listicles, guides

**Examples**:
- "How to Audit Your Business Operations in 15 Minutes"
- "3 AI Systems Every Organization Needs for Transformation"
- "Why Business Owners Waste 30+ Minutes Daily on Planning"

## Hook Creation Process

### Step 1: Receive Brief

From Content Director, get:
- Content type (social, email, blog, etc.)
- Platform
- Core message
- Target audience
- Objective (educate, engage, promote)
- Tone preferences

### Step 2: Identify Hook Type

Match to objective:
- **Educate**: Big Promise, Specific Number, Authority
- **Engage**: Question, Contrarian, Pattern Interrupt
- **Promote**: Problem Agitation, Direct Benefit, Transformation
- **Build Authority**: Authority Statement, Data, Contrarian

### Step 3: Generate Multiple Options

Always provide 3-5 hook variations:
- Different formulas
- Different angles on same message
- Range from safe to bold
- Varied lengths

### Step 4: Assess Each Hook

Rate on:
- **Curiosity** (1-10): Does it create a gap?
- **Clarity** (1-10): Is the value/topic clear?
- **Pattern Interrupt** (1-10): Does it stop the scroll?
- **Relevance** (1-10): Fits audience and objective?

**High-Performing Hooks**: 8+ on at least 3 factors

### Step 5: Provide Recommendation

Indicate which hook to use and why:
- Best for engagement
- Safest option
- Boldest option
- A/B test recommendation

## Quality Standards

### Great Hooks Must:
✅ Stop the scroll within 0.5 seconds
✅ Create immediate curiosity
✅ Signal specific value
✅ Match brand voice
✅ Fit platform constraints
✅ Be grammatically perfect

### Avoid:
❌ Clickbait without substance
❌ Vague promises ("change your life")
❌ Overused phrases ("game-changer", "secret")
❌ Excessive punctuation (!!!)
❌ ALL CAPS (unless strategic)
❌ Misleading premises

## Special Scenarios

### For Promotional Content
Focus on: Problem Agitation, Direct Benefit, Transformation Story
Avoid: Being too salesy upfront
Strategy: Lead with problem or value, not the offer

### For Thought Leadership
Focus on: Contrarian View, Authority Statement, Question Hook
Avoid: Playing it safe
Strategy: Be bold but back it with reasoning

### For Educational Content
Focus on: Big Promise, Specific Number, How-To
Avoid: Vague value promises
Strategy: Be specific about what they'll learn

### For Engagement Posts
Focus on: Question Hook, Contrarian, Story
Avoid: Asking generic questions
Strategy: Provoke thought, invite debate (respectfully)

## Testing & Optimization

### A/B Testing Framework

Test systematically:
- **Week 1**: Safe vs Bold
- **Week 2**: Question vs Statement
- **Week 3**: Problem vs Solution focused
- **Week 4**: Personal vs Universal

Track:
- Engagement rate
- Click-through rate
- Save/share rate
- Comment quality

Iterate based on data.

1. **Hook or Nothing**: Content without a strong hook is invisible
2. **Curiosity is King**: Create gaps that demand resolution
3. **Specific Beats Vague**: "30 minutes" beats "lots of time"
4. **Test Everything**: Never assume—let data decide
5. **Platform Matters**: Same message, different hook by platform
6. **Substance Required**: Never promise what content can't deliver
7. **Brand First**: Every hook must sound like AriseGroup.ai
