## Editing Process

### Step 1: Initial Read (Context)

Read content once WITHOUT editing:
- Understand objective
- Feel overall flow
- Identify major issues
- Note first impression

**Ask**: "Would I keep reading this if I saw it in my feed?"

---

### Step 2: Technical Edit (Line-by-Line)

Fix all technical errors:
- Grammar
- Spelling
- Punctuation
- Formatting
- Links

**Standard**: Flawless execution

---

### Step 3: Clarity Edit (Simplify)

Improve readability:

**Tighten**:
- Remove unnecessary words
- Shorten long sentences
- Break up paragraphs
- Add white space

**Clarify**:
- Replace vague with specific
- Replace abstract with concrete
- Replace jargon with plain language
- Add examples where needed

**Enhance Flow**:
- Improve transitions
- Fix logical gaps
- Ensure coherent argument

---

### Step 4: Voice Edit (Brand Consistency)

Align with AriseGroup.ai voice:

**Tone Check**:
- Too formal? ‚Üí Loosen up
- Too casual? ‚Üí Add professionalism
- Too soft? ‚Üí Be more direct
- Too harsh? ‚Üí Soften edges

**Style Check**:
- Vague benefits? ‚Üí Specify
- Hype language? ‚Üí Ground it
- Corporate jargon? ‚Üí Remove
- Overused phrases? ‚Üí Refresh

**Value Check**:
- Overpromising? ‚Üí Be honest
- Under-delivering? ‚Üí Add value
- Too salesy? ‚Üí Lead with value
- No clear benefit? ‚Üí Define it

---

### Step 5: Strategic Edit (Purpose)

Ensure strategic alignment:

**CTA Check**:
- Is it clear?
- Is it single (not multiple asks)?
- Is it compelling?
- Is it low-friction?
- Does it match objective?

**Value Check**:
- Does this provide real value?
- Is it actionable?
- Is it specific enough?
- Will audience find this useful?

**Platform Check**:
- Right length for platform?
- Formatted correctly?
- Hashtags appropriate?
- Timing recommendations included?

---

### Step 6: Final Polish (Excellence)

Final pass:
- Read aloud (catches awkward phrasing)
- Check all links
- Verify formatting
- Confirm no technical errors introduced
- One more brand voice check

**Ask**: "Is this our best work?"

## Common Issues & Fixes

### Issue: Weak Opening

**Problem**: First 1-2 lines don't hook

**Fix**:
- Start with question, bold statement, or surprising fact
- Cut throat-clearing ("In this post, I'll...")
- Create immediate curiosity
- Promise specific value

**Example**:

Before: "Today I want to talk about the importance of business systems."
After: "Most business owners waste 30+ minutes daily on planning. Here's the 60-second alternative."

---

### Issue: Vague Benefits

**Problem**: Claims aren't specific

**Fix**:
- Replace "boost productivity" with "save 10 hours weekly"
- Replace "improve operations" with "automate 3 recurring tasks"
- Replace "better results" with "40% revenue increase"
- Add concrete numbers and outcomes

**Example**:

Before: "Our system will make your business more productive."
After: "Reduces daily planning from 30 minutes to 60 seconds."

---

### Issue: Wall of Text

**Problem**: Paragraphs too long, no white space

**Fix**:
- Break paragraphs at 2-3 sentences
- Add line breaks between ideas
- Use bullets for lists
- Add visual breaks (‚Üí symbols)

---

### Issue: Multiple CTAs

**Problem**: Asking for too many actions

**Fix**:
- Choose ONE primary CTA
- Remove competing asks
- Make the action crystal clear
- Repeat same CTA if needed, don't add new ones

**Example**:

Before: "Visit our website, book a call, download our guide, and follow us on LinkedIn!"
After: "Book your free operations audit at AriseGroup.ai"

---

### Issue: Overpromising

**Problem**: Claims too big, not believable

**Fix**:
- Ground claims in reality
- Add qualifiers where honest
- Show process, not just results
- Be transparent about what's required

**Example**:

Before: "Transform your entire business in 7 days!"
After: "Built this system in 1 day. Still refining it, but already saving 30 minutes daily."

---

### Issue: Off-Brand Tone

**Problem**: Doesn't sound like AriseGroup.ai

**Fix**:
- Remove hype language
- Add military-discipline angle (but not overwhelming)
- Focus on systems over motivation
- Be direct and honest
- Show, don't just tell

### Give Specific Feedback

Bad: "This needs work"
Good: "The opening doesn't hook. Try leading with the problem: 'Most businesses waste 30+ minutes on planning.'"

Bad: "CTA is weak"

## Special Scenarios

### Editing Promotional Content

**Extra vigilance on**:
- Not too pushy or salesy
- Value delivered before ask
- CTA clear but not aggressive
- Urgency only if real
- Proof or credibility included

### Editing Educational Content

**Focus on**:
- Actionability (can they use this?)
- Specificity (concrete examples?)
- Completeness (all steps included?)
- Clarity (jargon-free?)

### Editing Personal Stories

**Balance**:
- Vulnerability vs oversharing
- Personal vs universal lesson
- Specific vs rambling
- Authentic vs performative
