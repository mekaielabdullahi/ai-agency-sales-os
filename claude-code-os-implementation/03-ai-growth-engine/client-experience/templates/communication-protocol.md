## Communication Channels

### Primary Channel: Notion Project Portal
**Use for:**
- Project status tracking
- Deliverable sharing
- Documentation
- Async updates and questions

**Access:** Shared with client during onboarding
**Updated:** Weekly minimum, more as needed

---

### Secondary Channel: Email
**Use for:**
- Direct questions requiring response
- Formal approvals and confirmations
- Urgent items
- Weekly status updates

**Response Time:** Within 24 hours (business days)

---

### Scheduled Calls
**Use for:**
- Kickoff
- Architecture review
- UAT & Training
- Go-live
- Weekly syncs (optional)

**All calls:**
- Scheduled in advance
- Calendar invites sent
- Zoom links provided
- Agendas sent 24 hours prior

---

### Emergency Channel
**Use for:** Production issues ONLY (after go-live)
**Method:** Phone call to project lead
**Response Time:** 2 hours

**NOT for:** Status updates, general questions, non-urgent items

---

| Priority | Response Time | Examples |

| **Low** | 48 hours | Nice-to-haves, future planning, optional items |

**Business hours:** Monday-Friday, 9am-5pm [Timezone]
**After hours:** Emergency items only

| Approval requests | Within 2-3 business days |

## Emergency Communication Protocol

### Definition of Emergency

An emergency is a **production issue** affecting client operations:
- Automation system down or not functioning
- Data loss or corruption
- Security breach or unauthorized access
- Critical workflow failure affecting customers

**NOT emergencies:** Questions, feature requests, training needs, minor bugs

---

### Emergency Response Process

**For Client:**
1. Call project lead directly: [Phone]
2. If no answer, email with subject: **URGENT - Production Issue - [Client Name]**
3. Describe issue clearly and impact on business

**From Arise Team:**
1. Acknowledge within 2 hours
2. Provide initial assessment within 4 hours
3. Provide resolution or workaround within 24 hours
4. Post-incident report within 48 hours

---

## Client Communication Principles

### 1. Clarity Over Speed
Don't rush responses. A clear, thoughtful answer is better than a fast, confusing one.

---

### 2. Proactive > Reactive
Don't wait for clients to ask. Anticipate questions and address them upfront.

**Bad:** Wait for client to ask "Where are we with the project?"
**Good:** Send weekly updates before they have to ask.

---

### 3. Honesty Always
If there's a delay, own it. If you don't know, say so.

**Bad:** "Everything's fine!" (when it's not)
**Good:** "We hit a snag with [X]. Here's our plan to fix it: [Y]."

---

### 4. No Jargon
Translate technical concepts into business outcomes.

**Bad:** "The webhook endpoint is returning 401 errors."
**Good:** "The connection between your website and CRM isn't working. We're fixing it now."

---

### 5. Assume Positive Intent
If a client sounds frustrated, they're stressedâ€”not attacking you. Respond with empathy.

**Bad:** Defensive response
**Good:** "I understand this is frustrating. Let me make it right."

---

### 6. Document Everything
If it's important, put it in writing (email or Notion). Don't rely on verbal-only agreements.

---

### 7. Over-Communicate Timeline Changes
Clients hate surprises. If a timeline shifts, tell them immediatelyâ€”not a week later.

---

### 8. Celebrate Wins
Don't just communicate problems. Celebrate milestones, progress, and successes.

---

## Communication Cadence by Project Phase

### Week 1-2 (Onboarding & Architecture):
- Frequency: High (daily or every other day)
- Method: Email + calls
- Purpose: Gather information, build trust

### Weeks 3-5 (Build Phase):
- Frequency: Weekly
- Method: Email updates
- Purpose: Keep client informed, maintain confidence

### Weeks 5-6 (Testing & Training):
- Frequency: High (2-3x/week)
- Method: Email + calls
- Purpose: Prepare for launch, train team

### Weeks 6-8 (Stabilization):
- Frequency: Medium (2x/week)
- Method: Email + monitoring reports
- Purpose: Ensure stability, optimize

### Post-Launch (Ongoing):
- Frequency: Monthly (unless support retainer)
- Method: Email check-ins
- Purpose: Maintain relationship, identify upsell opportunities

### If Client Unresponsive

**Day 1:** Initial request sent
**Day 3:** Friendly reminder sent
**Day 5:** Second reminder with timeline impact noted
**Day 7:** Escalation email to client + internal team
