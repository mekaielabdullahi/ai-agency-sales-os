**Purpose**: Extract key information from clients to build actionable profiles

## üìã SECTION 1: BUSINESS OVERVIEW
**Purpose**: Understand what they do and how they operate
**When**: Initial contact or discovery call

### Basic Questions:

1. **"Walk me through what [Company] does - specifically, how do you make money?"**
   - Extracts: Business model, revenue streams, core offerings
   - Follow-up: "What's your primary offering vs secondary?"

2. **"Who are your ideal customers/clients?"**
   - Extracts: Target market, customer profile, positioning
   - Follow-up: "What makes someone a perfect fit vs wrong fit?"

3. **"How big is your team, and how is it structured?"**
   - Extracts: Company size, org structure, decision-making
   - Follow-up: "Who handles [specific function relevant to AI]?"

4. **"What makes you different from your competitors?"**
   - Extracts: Unique value prop, competitive positioning
   - Follow-up: "What do customers choose you over them for?"

5. **"Are you growing, maintaining, or scaling back right now?"**
   - Extracts: Growth stage, trajectory, urgency
   - Follow-up: "What's driving that growth/challenge?"

## üîç SECTION 2: CURRENT STATE & OPERATIONS
**Purpose**: Understand how they currently work
**When**: Discovery call

### Process Questions:

6. **"Walk me through a typical [project/sale/customer journey] from start to finish."**
   - Extracts: Workflow, bottlenecks, complexity
   - Follow-up: "Where do things typically slow down or break?"

7. **"What tools and systems are you currently using?"**
   - Extracts: Tech stack, existing systems, integration needs
   - Follow-up: "What's working well? What's frustrating?"

8. **"How do you currently handle [specific function relevant to AI]?"**
   - Examples: lead generation, customer service, operations, etc.
   - Extracts: Current process, manual vs automated
   - Follow-up: "How much time does that take per day/week?"

9. **"What does your team spend most of their time on?"**
   - Extracts: Time allocation, priorities, inefficiencies
   - Follow-up: "What would you rather they spend time on?"

10. **"How do you track performance/results right now?"**
    - Extracts: Metrics, KPIs, visibility into operations
    - Follow-up: "Do you feel like you have good visibility or are you flying blind?"

## üö® SECTION 3: PROBLEMS & PAIN POINTS
**Purpose**: Uncover explicit and implicit problems
**When**: Discovery call (CRITICAL SECTION)

### Problem Identification Questions:

11. **"What's the biggest challenge you're facing right now in [their business area]?"**
    - Extracts: Top-of-mind problem, urgency
    - Follow-up: "How long has this been a problem?"

12. **"If you could wave a magic wand and fix one thing about your operations, what would it be?"**
    - Extracts: Dream solution, priority problem
    - Follow-up: "What would that unlock for you?"

13. **"What keeps you up at night about the business?"**
    - Extracts: Deep concerns, risk areas
    - Follow-up: "What's the worst-case scenario if you don't solve it?"

14. **"Where are you losing the most money/time/opportunities right now?"**
    - Extracts: Quantifiable pain, financial impact
    - Follow-up: "What's that costing you per month/year?"

15. **"What are you currently doing manually that you wish was automated?"**
    - Extracts: Automation opportunities, repetitive tasks
    - Follow-up: "How much time would automation save?"

16. **"What bottleneck is preventing you from growing faster?"**
    - Extracts: Constraint, growth blocker
    - Follow-up: "If we broke that constraint, what's the next one?"

17. **"What customer complaints or issues do you hear most often?"**
    - Extracts: Service gaps, customer pain points
    - Follow-up: "How are you currently handling those?"

18. **"Where do deals typically fall apart in your sales process?"**
    - Extracts: Sales friction, conversion blockers
    - Follow-up: "What do you think is causing that?"

## üí∞ SECTION 4: FINANCIAL & ROI
**Purpose**: Understand budget, value, and ROI expectations
**When**: Discovery call or qualification stage

### Value Questions:

19. **"What's the cost of NOT solving [problem you identified]?"**
    - Extracts: Financial impact, urgency, pain severity
    - Follow-up: "How is that impacting your bottom line?"

20. **"If we could [solve X problem], what would that be worth to you?"**
    - Extracts: Value perception, ROI expectations
    - Follow-up: "In terms of revenue, time saved, or cost reduction?"

21. **"Have you budgeted for solving this, or is this exploratory?"**
    - Extracts: Budget authority, buying readiness
    - Follow-up: "What's your typical budget range for [solution type]?"

22. **"What ROI would you need to see to make this a no-brainer investment?"**
    - Extracts: Decision criteria, success metrics
    - Follow-up: "Is that measured in revenue, time, efficiency, or something else?"

23. **"Have you tried to solve this before? What happened?"**
    - Extracts: Past attempts, why they failed, objections
    - Follow-up: "What would make this time different?"

## ‚è∞ SECTION 5: TIMELINE & URGENCY
**Purpose**: Understand decision timeline and urgency
**When**: Qualification stage

### Timing Questions:

24. **"When do you need this solved by?"**
    - Extracts: Timeline, urgency level
    - Follow-up: "What happens if it's not solved by then?"

25. **"What's driving the timeline? Is there a specific event or deadline?"**
    - Extracts: External pressures, forcing function
    - Follow-up: "Is that timeline flexible or firm?"

26. **"Are you actively evaluating solutions right now, or is this early-stage research?"**
    - Extracts: Buying stage, competition for deal
    - Follow-up: "What's your evaluation process look like?"

27. **"How quickly can you make a decision once you find the right solution?"**
    - Extracts: Decision speed, approval process
    - Follow-up: "Who else needs to be involved in that decision?"

## üë• SECTION 6: STAKEHOLDERS & DECISION MAKING
**Purpose**: Map the decision-making landscape
**When**: Discovery or qualification stage

### Decision Questions:

28. **"Who else would need to be involved in a decision like this?"**
    - Extracts: Stakeholders, influencers, approvers
    - Follow-up: "What does each person care about most?"

29. **"What's your typical process for evaluating and purchasing [solution type]?"**
    - Extracts: Buying process, timeline, steps
    - Follow-up: "What are the key milestones in that process?"

30. **"Who has final say on budget approval?"**
    - Extracts: Budget authority, decision maker
    - Follow-up: "What criteria do they use to approve?"

31. **"Is there anyone who might push back on this internally?"**
    - Extracts: Potential blockers, objections
    - Follow-up: "What are their concerns typically?"

## üéØ SECTION 7: SUCCESS CRITERIA
**Purpose**: Define what success looks like for them
**When**: Discovery call

### Outcome Questions:

32. **"What does success look like for you 6 months after implementing a solution?"**
    - Extracts: Goals, KPIs, expectations
    - Follow-up: "How would you measure that?"

33. **"If we worked together, what would make you think 'this was the best decision ever'?"**
    - Extracts: Ideal outcome, decision validation
    - Follow-up: "What would make you regret it?"

34. **"What specific results or metrics would you need to see?"**
    - Extracts: Success metrics, quantifiable goals
    - Follow-up: "What's the baseline now vs where you want to be?"

35. **"Beyond the numbers, what qualitative improvements are you hoping for?"**
    - Extracts: Soft benefits (peace of mind, less stress, better sleep)
    - Follow-up: "How important is that compared to the quantifiable ROI?"

## üìä QUESTION FRAMEWORKS

### THE LAYERED QUESTION TECHNIQUE

**How to use**: Start broad, drill down based on answers

**Example**:
1. **Layer 1** (Broad): "What's your biggest operational challenge?"
   - Answer: "Managing our project pipeline"

2. **Layer 2** (Specific): "Tell me more about your project pipeline - what specifically is challenging?"
   - Answer: "We lose track of projects, miss deadlines"

3. **Layer 3** (Quantify): "How often does that happen, and what's the impact?"
   - Answer: "2-3 times per month, costs us about $10K in rushed work"

4. **Layer 4** (Vision): "If you had perfect visibility and never missed a deadline, what would change?"
   - Answer: "We could take on 30% more projects confidently"

**Now you have**: Problem (pipeline management), Frequency (2-3x/month), Cost ($10K/month), Opportunity (30% growth)

## üí° PRO TIPS

### 1. **Listen More Than You Talk**
- 80% listening, 20% talking
- Questions are about THEM, not YOU

### 2. **Follow the Energy**
- When they get animated, dig deeper
- "Tell me more about that..."
- "Why is that important to you?"

### 3. **Silence is Powerful**
- Ask question
- Shut up
- Let them fill the silence
- Best insights come after pauses

### 4. **Take Notes Visibly**
- "Let me write that down - this is important"
- Shows you value what they're saying
- Captures exact language they use

### 5. **Use Their Words Back**
- Client says: "Our onboarding is painfully slow"
- You say: "Tell me more about the onboarding being painfully slow..."
- Don't translate - use their exact phrasing

### 6. **The "Why" Chain**
- Ask "why" 3-5 times to get to root cause
- Problem: "We're not closing enough deals"
- Why? "We're slow to respond to leads"
- Why? "Our CRM doesn't notify us fast enough"
- Why? "It's not integrated with our forms"
- ‚Üí REAL PROBLEM: Integration gap, not sales skills
