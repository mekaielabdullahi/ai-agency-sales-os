## ROLE FRAMEWORK

### The 6 Core Roles (Your Agreement)

**ROLE 1: ORIGINATOR**
- **Description:** Brought the lead, owns relationship long-term
- **Suggested Range:** 15-20%
- **Responsibilities:**
  - Sourced the lead from their network
  - Made initial contact
  - Qualified basic fit
  - Long-term relationship ownership (see Section 5 for details)
- **Who:** Whoever brought the lead (could be any of the 4)
- **Rules:**
  - Only ONE Originator per project
  - Can't be retroactively changed
  - Ownership documented in Notion at project start

---

**ROLE 2: CLIENT LEAD**
- **Description:** Primary client communication, scoping, project management
- **Suggested Range:** 15-20%
- **Responsibilities:**
  - Runs discovery calls (Q1-Q5)
  - Creates proposal with team input
  - Primary point of contact with client
  - Project management and coordination
  - Ensures delivery quality
  - Client communication throughout project
  - Owns client relationship during active project
- **Who:** Usually Chris (sales strength) or the Originator
- **Rules:**
  - Only ONE Client Lead per project
  - Has final decision-making authority if team can't reach consensus
  - Responsible for client satisfaction

---

**ROLE 3: TECHNICAL LEAD**
- **Description:** Primary hands-on execution
- **Suggested Range:** 30-40%
- **Responsibilities:**
  - Does majority of technical work
  - Architecture and design decisions
  - Primary builder/implementer
  - Code/system quality ownership
  - Technical decisions
  - Automatically becomes Support Owner (unless negotiated otherwise)
- **Who:** Matthew, Trent, or Mekaiel depending on project type
- **Rules:**
  - Only ONE Technical Lead per project
  - Gets highest percentage (they do most work)
  - Default Support Owner for 30 days post-delivery

---

**ROLE 4: TECHNICAL SUPPORT**
- **Description:** Secondary execution, review, QA, assistance
- **Suggested Range:** 15-25%
- **Responsibilities:**
  - Assists Technical Lead
  - Code review and QA
  - Secondary implementation work
  - Integration support
  - Testing and validation
  - Backup if Technical Lead blocked
- **Who:** Second dev (if Matthew is Tech Lead, Trent is Support, or vice versa)
- **Rules:**
  - Can have 0-2 Technical Support roles per project
  - Percentage scales with contribution level
  - Clear scope definition before project starts

---

**ROLE 5: SUPPORT OWNER**
- **Description:** Responsible for 30-day post-delivery support
- **Suggested Range:** 5-10%
- **Responsibilities:**
  - Bug fixes within 30 days
  - User questions and training
  - Minor adjustments
  - Troubleshooting
  - Ensuring client success during support window
- **Who:** Default = Technical Lead (who built it)
- **Rules:**
  - Can be separate from Technical Lead if negotiated
  - If separate, reduces Technical Lead % and adds Support Owner %
  - Responsibility starts at delivery, ends 30 days later
  - NOT responsible for major new features (those are new paid work)

---

**ROLE 6: LEARNING/PROCESS**
- **Description:** Observing, documenting, building internal systems
- **Suggested Range:** 3-7% (recommendation - see expanded section below)
- **Responsibilities:**
  - Shadow the project to learn
  - Document processes and learnings
  - Build reusable templates/systems
  - Post-project retrospective
  - Create case studies or internal knowledge
  - Improve collaboration efficiency
- **Who:** Rotates - whoever needs to learn that type of project
- **Rules:**
  - Optional role (not required on every project)
  - Percentage comes from team vote per project
  - Can't interfere with delivery
  - Deliverable = documented learnings/systems

#### Q1: What exact percentages feel fair for each role?

**Complex Projects ($7K-$10K, multi-system, custom development):**

**Per-Project Adjustment Process:**
1. Client Lead proposes split based on project complexity

3. Any objections? Discuss and adjust
4. Vote to approve (simple majority)
5. Lock it in before work starts

**Starting Point (Recommend for First 3-5 Projects):**
Use Option A (Standard Project, No Learning Role) with these exact percentages:
- Originator: 15%
- Client Lead: 18%
- Technical Lead: 40% (includes Support Owner)

**After 5 projects, review:**
- Are these working?
- Adjust based on actual experience
- Update this framework

**Why:**
- Prevents one person taking >50% when they don't do technical work
- Keeps incentives aligned (everyone wants to collaborate)
- Technical work is hardest, should be rewarded most

**Rule:**
**If Originator = Client Lead (same person):**
- Combined cap: 35% total (not 15% + 18% = 33%)
- Rationale: Role overlap reduces total effort
- Example: Chris brings lead AND runs project â†’ 35% (not 40%)

**If Originator â‰  Client Lead (different people):**
- No cap, each gets their role percentage
- Example: Mekaiel brings lead (15%), Chris runs project (18%) â†’ Total 33% to sales/PM

**Edge Case:**
If Originator + Client Lead + Technical Lead = same person (solo project):
- Cap at 75-80% (leave 20-25% for support roles)
- OR just do it solo and keep 100% (but miss collaboration benefits)

## PAYMENT & CASH FLOW MANAGEMENT

### Your Agreement
- Collaborators paid within 7-10 days of client payment receipt
- Everyone shares cash flow risk equally
- If client 45+ days late, group decides collectively

---

### Payment Process (Step-by-Step)

**Step 1: Client Pays**
- Payment goes to designated "Banker" for that project
- Banker = Usually Client Lead or Originator (decided at project start)
- Goes into their business bank account

**Step 2: Banker Notifies Team**
- Posts in #payments Slack channel within 24 hours
- "Project [Name] - Received $X,XXX on [Date]. Distributing per agreed split."

**Step 3: Banker Distributes (Within 7-10 Days)**
- Sends payments to each collaborator per agreed percentages
- Wire, Venmo, Zelle, or check (whatever's easiest)
- Includes note: "AriseGroup Project [Name] - [Role] - [X%]"

**Step 4: Recipients Invoice Banker**
- Each person sends invoice to Banker for their own records
- Invoice from their LLC/sole prop
- Line item: "AriseGroup Project [Name] - [Role] services - $X,XXX"
- This creates proper paper trail for taxes

**Step 5: Reconciliation**
- Banker updates shared spreadsheet (see templates below)
- Status: "Paid" with date
- Everyone confirms receipt in Slack

---

### Cash Flow Risk Sharing

**Rule: Everyone shares risk equally**

**What this means:**

**If client pays on time (Net 15-30):**
- Banker distributes within 7-10 days
- No issues, standard process

**If client is 15-45 days late:**
- Banker sends reminder to client
- Banker does NOT distribute until payment received
- Nobody gets paid until client pays (shared risk)
- Client Lead follows up aggressively

**If client is 45+ days late:**
- Emergency team call (all 4 partners)
- Discuss options:
  - Send final notice?
  - Collections process?
  - Write off as bad debt?
  - Legal action?
- Vote on next steps (unanimous for legal action)
- If writing off: Everyone takes equal % loss
  - Example: $8K project, client doesn't pay, everyone eats the loss proportionally

**If client only pays partial amount:**
- Example: Agreed $8K, client pays $6K and disputes $2K
- Team decides: Accept $6K and distribute per %? Or fight for full amount?
- If accepted: Everyone gets their % of $6K (not $8K)

**Protection Against Cash Flow Risk:**
- 50% upfront payment (before work starts)
- 50% on delivery (before 30-day support)
- This limits max exposure to 50% of project value

---

### Bad Debt Protocol

**If client completely doesn't pay:**

1. **Client Lead** exhausts all collection attempts
2. **Team meeting** to decide: pursue or write off
3. **If write off:** Document as lesson learned
4. **Nobody blames Client Lead** (we all agreed to take client)
5. **Update process** to prevent similar situation

**Example:**
- $8K project, client disappears after delivery
- Already received 50% upfront ($4K)
- Missing 50% ($4K)
- Team votes to write off the $4K
- Each person loses their % of $4K:
  - Originator (15%): Lost $600
  - Client Lead (18%): Lost $720
  - Technical Lead (40%): Lost $1,600
  - Technical Support (20%): Lost $800
  - Support Owner (7%): Lost $280

**Learnings captured, process improved, move forward.**

## RISK & LIABILITY SHARING

### Your 2 Questions (ANSWERED)

---

#### Q13: If project goes sideways, how do you share the downside?

**RECOMMENDATION:** Proportional to revenue share, with Client Lead taking reputational responsibility

**Framework:**

**FINANCIAL RISK (Shared Proportionally):**

**If client doesn't pay:**
- Everyone loses their % of unpaid amount
- Example: $8K project, client pays $0, everyone eats loss per their %

**If project requires more work than scoped:**
- Client Lead calls team meeting
- Options:
  1. **Eat the cost** (everyone works extra for same pay)
  2. **Renegotiate with client** (try to get more $)
  3. **Cut scope** (deliver less, keep original price)
- Team votes on approach
- If eating cost: Everyone contributes proportional to their original %

**If client demands refund:**
- Team meeting to assess validity
- If legitimate quality issue: Refund proportionally (everyone returns their %)
- If client just changed mind: Offer to fix, don't refund
- If legal threat: Decide collectively (unanimous vote for legal action)

---

**REPUTATIONAL RISK (Client Lead Bears Most)**

**Client Lead's responsibility:**
- Ensure delivery quality
- Manage client expectations
- Communicate issues early
- Make final call on client-facing decisions

**But:** Team supports Client Lead
- If Client Lead is drowning, team helps
- Post-mortems are blameless (focus on process, not person)
- Don't throw Client Lead under bus to client

---

**LIABILITY RISK (Individual + Collective)**

**Individual Liability:**
- Each person's LLC provides some protection
- Your own business insurance covers you
