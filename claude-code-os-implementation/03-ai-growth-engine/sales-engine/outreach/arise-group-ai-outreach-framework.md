# Arise Group AI Outreach Framework
**The 5 Questions Method for Closing Any Lead**

---

## üéØ Framework Philosophy

**Core Principle**: You don't close leads by pitching solutions. You close leads by uncovering problems, quantifying pain, and letting prospects sell themselves.

**The Arise Advantage**: While competitors pitch features, we diagnose business problems. While they talk about AI tools, we talk about money saved and problems solved.

**Result**: Higher close rates, better-qualified clients, premium pricing, and zero price resistance.

---

## üß† The 5 Questions System

Every conversation‚Äîemail, call, DM, meeting‚Äîflows through these 5 questions in order:

### **Q1: What is the biggest problem in your business right now?**
**Purpose**: Problem discovery & qualification
**What you're learning**: Is there a real problem? Do they recognize it? Is it relevant to what we solve?

### **Q2: How much is this problem costing the business per month?**
**Purpose**: Financial quantification & pricing anchor
**What you're learning**: What's the budget ceiling? How urgent is this? Do they understand the real cost?

### **Q3: How long have you had this problem for?**
**Purpose**: Timeline & urgency assessment
**What you're learning**: Is this chronic pain or new? Have they tried to solve it? Why haven't they fixed it yet?

### **Q4: If you do nothing, what does the next 6 to 12 months look like?**
**Purpose**: Consequence amplification & urgency creation
**What you're learning**: Do they understand the stakes? Is inaction more expensive than action?

### **Q5: If I had a magic wand and could build the perfect solution to fix this problem, what would that look like?**
**Purpose**: Solution design & proposal blueprint
**What you're learning**: What do THEY want (not what we want to sell)? What does success look like to them?

---

## üìã How to Use the Framework

### **RULE #1: Questions Flow in Order**
Never skip ahead. Each question builds on the previous one.
- Q1 without Q2 = no pricing context
- Q2 without Q3 = no urgency
- Q4 without Q5 = fear without hope
- Q5 without Q1-Q4 = solution without problem

### **RULE #2: Listen More Than You Talk**
Your job in questions 1-5 is to EXTRACT information, not provide it.
- Ask the question
- Shut up
- Let them talk
- Ask clarifying questions
- Repeat back what you heard
- Move to next question

### **RULE #3: Their Answers ARE Your Proposal**
By the time you reach Q5, you should know:
- The problem (Q1)
- What they'll pay (Q2)
- How urgent it is (Q3)
- Why they must act now (Q4)
- Exactly what to build/propose (Q5)

**You're not pitching‚Äîyou're prescribing based on their diagnosis.**

## üö´ Common Mistakes & How to Avoid Them

### **MISTAKE #1: Pitching Too Early**
**What it looks like**: Asking Q1, then immediately pitching your solution
**Why it fails**: You don't know enough yet. No cost context, no urgency, no magic wand vision
**Fix**: Discipline. Do NOT pitch until you have answers to all 5 questions

### **MISTAKE #2: Not Quantifying Cost (Q2)**
**What it looks like**: They say "it's a big problem" but you don't get a dollar amount
**Why it fails**: You can't price your solution. You can't calculate ROI. You can't create urgency
**Fix**: Push for a number. "Ballpark is fine‚Äîis it costing $1K/month? $10K? $50K?"

### **MISTAKE #3: Skipping Q4 (Consequence)**
**What it looks like**: Going from Q3 straight to Q5
**Why it fails**: No emotional urgency. They don't feel the pain of inaction
**Fix**: Always ask Q4. It's the difference between "nice to have" and "must have now"

### **MISTAKE #4: Not Using Their Language in Q5**
**What it looks like**: They describe their magic wand solution, then you pitch something different
**Why it fails**: You're selling what YOU want, not what THEY want
**Fix**: Your proposal should use their exact words from Q5. Copy/paste their language

### **MISTAKE #5: Asking All 5 Questions in One Message**
**What it looks like**: Email with all 5 questions in bullet points
**Why it fails**: Overwhelming. Feels like a survey. Low response rate
**Fix**: One or two questions per touchpoint. Build the conversation over time

## üéì Training Exercises

### **Exercise 1: Role Play - Full Sequence**
**Setup**: One person is the prospect (given a problem scenario). One person is the Arise rep.
**Goal**: Get through all 5 questions naturally in a 10-minute conversation
**Debrief**: Did the rep listen? Did they pitch too early? Did they use the prospect's language?

### **Exercise 2: Email Sequencing**
**Setup**: Write a 4-email sequence for a hypothetical prospect
**Goal**: Each email should advance through questions 1-5
**Debrief**: Is each email short? Does it ask only 1-2 questions? Would you respond to this?

### **Exercise 3: Objection Handling with Q4**
**Setup**: Prospect says "I need to think about it"
**Goal**: Use Q4 (consequence) to create urgency without being pushy
**Example Response**: "Totally fair. Just to clarify‚Äîyou mentioned that if nothing changes, [their Q4 consequence]. How much longer can you afford to wait?"

### **Exercise 4: Pricing from Q2**
**Setup**: Given a Q2 answer ("This is costing us about $20K/month"), calculate your price
**Goal**: Price at 30-50% of monthly problem cost
**Answer**: $6K-$10K/month is the range
**Rationale**: If they're losing $20K/month and you charge $8K/month, they net $12K/month savings = easy ROI

## üìû Real Example Walkthrough

**Scenario**: Inbound lead from LinkedIn - Marketing Director at B2B SaaS company

**Q1: "What's the biggest problem in your business right now?"**
**Prospect**: "Our sales team is drowning in unqualified leads. We're generating volume but conversion rates are terrible."

**Q2: "How much is this costing you per month?"**
**Prospect**: "Hard to say exactly, but we're spending about $30K/month on lead gen, and our sales team wastes probably 60% of their time on bad fits. That's like 3 full-time salaries... so maybe $45K total wasted per month?"

**Q3: "How long has this been an issue?"**
**Prospect**: "About 8 months. It got worse when we scaled our ad spend."

**Q4: "If you do nothing, what does the next 6-12 months look like?"**
**Prospect**: "Honestly? We'll probably have to hire more SDRs to sift through the garbage, which means more overhead. Or we cut ad spend and miss our growth targets. Lose-lose."

**Q5: "Magic wand‚Äîwhat's the perfect solution?"**
**Prospect**: "I wish we had an AI system that could pre-qualify leads before they ever hit the sales team. Like, answer their basic questions, figure out if they're a fit, book qualified meetings only. That would be a game-changer."

**Now You Pitch**:
```
"Okay, so based on what you just told me:

You're spending $45K/month on unqualified leads and wasted sales time.
This has been going on for 8 months.
If nothing changes, you're looking at hiring more people or missing targets.
What you really want is an AI system that pre-qualifies leads and only books qualified meetings.

Good news‚Äîwe built exactly that for [similar SaaS company].

Here's how it works:
[Describe AI phone agent + qualification system using their language]

The investment is $15K/month.

Given that you're currently wasting $45K/month, this nets you $30K in savings‚Äîplus your sales team only talks to qualified prospects.

And we can have this live in 2 weeks.

Make sense?"
```

**Why This Works**:
- Used their exact problem (Q1)
- Anchored price to their cost (Q2: $45K ‚Üí charge $15K)
- Referenced their timeline (Q3: 8 months of pain)
- Amplified urgency (Q4: hire more people or miss targets)
- Built solution from their vision (Q5: AI pre-qualification)
- **They literally told you what to build and what to charge.**

## üí° Advanced Tactics

### **Tactic 1: The "Pattern Interrupt" Opening**
Instead of "What's your biggest problem?" try:
- "Most [their role] tell me their biggest problem is [common problem]. Is that you, or is it something else?"
- Creates pattern interrupt + shows you know their world

### **Tactic 2: The "Assumption Close" on Q5**
After they describe their magic wand solution:
- "Got it. So if we could build that for you and have it live in [timeframe], that solves this, right?"
- Gets micro-commitment before you pitch

### **Tactic 3: The "Cost Amplification" on Q3**
When they tell you timeline (e.g., "2 years"):
- "So if this is costing you [Q2 amount] per month, that's [multiply by 24 months] you've lost so far?"
- Makes the pain concrete and large

### **Tactic 4: The "Consequence Ladder" on Q4**
If their Q4 answer is weak:
- "And if that happens, then what?"
- "And if that continues, then what?"
- Keep going until you hit real emotional pain

### **Tactic 5: The "Budget Qualifier" Before Q5**
Before asking Q5, insert:
- "Quick sanity check‚Äîif the perfect solution cost [30% of Q2], would that be in budget?"
- Qualifies budget before you invest time in proposal
