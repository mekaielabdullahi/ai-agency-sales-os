# Discovery Call Script & Framework

## Call Objective
Uncover operational bottlenecks and position the AI Audit as the essential next step in their transformation.

**Duration:** 15-25 minutes
**Format:** Consultative, solution-driven

---

## Pre-Call Preparation (5 minutes before call)

- [ ] Review ICP score and qualification notes
- [ ] Research company website and recent activity
- [ ] Identify 2-3 likely pain points based on industry
- [ ] Have note-taking template open and ready
- [ ] Confirm tech setup (audio, recording if permitted)
- [ ] Review their LinkedIn profile
- [ ] Have proposal template ready for customization

---

## Call Structure & Timing

| Phase | Duration | Objective |
|-------|----------|-----------|
| 1. Opening & Rapport | 2 min | Establish professional, friendly tone |
| 2. Business Overview | 3-5 min | Understand operations and strengths |
| 3. Pain Points Discovery | 5-7 min | Uncover friction and inefficiency |
| 4. Current Workflows | 3 min | Map customer journey |
| 5. Automation Opportunity | 3 min | Present 2-4 high-impact opportunities |
| 6. AI Audit Pitch | 2 min | Position audit as logical next step |
| 7. Next Steps | 2 min | Book roadmap call |

**Total:** 20-25 minutes

### Opening Statement

> "Hi [Name], thanks so much for taking the time today. I'm [Your Name] with Arise Group AI. I've done some research on [Company Name], and I'm excited to learn more about what you're building."

> "I have about 20 minutes blocked for us today. My goal is to understand how your business operates, where you might be experiencing bottlenecks, and see if AI automation could help you scale more efficiently. Does that sound good?"

## Phase 2: Business Overview (3-5 minutes)

### Objective
Understand what they do, who they serve, and current operational strengths.

### Key Questions

**Opening Question:**
> "To start, can you give me a high-level overview of what [Company Name] does and who you serve?"

**Follow-up Questions (Select 2-3 based on response):**

1. **Team Structure:**
   > "How big is your team right now, and how is it structured?"

2. **Customer Base:**
   > "What does your typical customer look like?"

3. **Service Delivery:**
   > "Walk me through what happens after someone becomes a customer. What's that journey like?"

4. **Growth Trajectory:**
   > "How has business been over the past 6-12 months?"

5. **Volume:**
   > "Roughly how many new leads or customers are you handling per month?"

### Active Listening
- Take detailed notes
- Identify potential automation opportunities as they speak
- Note any pain points mentioned (even casually)

## Phase 3: Pain Points Discovery (5-7 minutes) â­ CRITICAL PHASE

### Objective
This is the MOST important part of the call. Systematically uncover friction and inefficiency using targeted questions.

### The 4 Core Pain Point Questions

#### Question 1: The Breakdown Question
> "Where are things breaking down operationally right now?"

**Listen for:**
- Manual processes consuming time
- Tasks falling through the cracks
- Team overwhelm
- Customer complaints
- Revenue leaks

---

#### Question 2: The Friction Question
> "Where do you feel the most friction or inefficiency in your day-to-day?"

**Listen for:**
- Repetitive tasks
- Context-switching
- Data entry
- Double work
- Communication gaps

---

#### Question 3: The Shouldn't Be Question
> "What's happening inside the business that shouldn't be happening?"

**Listen for:**
- Missed opportunities
- Slow response times
- Scheduling chaos
- Lost leads
- Poor customer experience

---

#### Question 4: The Bottleneck Question
> "What's the biggest bottleneck preventing you from scaling right now?"

**Listen for:**
- Capacity constraints
- Time limitations
- Process inefficiencies
- Team bandwidth issues

---

## Phase 5: Automation Opportunity Frame (3 minutes)

### Objective
Present 2-4 high-impact automation opportunities that directly address their pain points.

### The Bridge Statement

> "Based on what you've shared, I'm seeing a few clear opportunities where automation could make a significant impact for you."

### Present Opportunities (Choose 2-4)

**Template for Each Opportunity:**
> "First, [Specific Pain Point]. We could build [Specific Automation Solution] that would [Specific Outcome]."

**Examples:**

**Opportunity 1: Lead Management**
> "You mentioned leads are falling through the cracks. We could build an AI-powered lead qualification and routing system that automatically captures every lead, qualifies them based on your criteria, and routes them to the right personâ€”with automatic follow-up sequences."

**Opportunity 2: Appointment Setting**
> "You mentioned scheduling is chaotic. We could implement an AI appointment setter that handles booking, sends automated reminders, reduces no-shows, and syncs everything to your calendar and CRM automatically."

**Opportunity 3: Customer Communication**
> "You mentioned response times are slow. We could deploy an AI voice agent or chatbot that handles initial customer inquiries 24/7, answers common questions, and escalates complex issues to your team."

**Opportunity 4: CRM Automation**
> "You mentioned your team is spending hours on data entry. We could build workflow automation that captures data from all your toolsâ€”website, social media, booking systemâ€”and automatically syncs it to your CRM."

**Opportunity 5: Follow-up Sequences**
> "You mentioned leads go cold. We could create intelligent follow-up sequences that nurture leads automatically via email and SMS until they're ready to buy."

### Quantify the Impact

> "Based on what you've told me, I estimate this could save your team [X hours per week] and potentially recover [Y% more leads/revenue]."

## Phase 6: The AI Audit Pitch (2 minutes) â­ CRITICAL

### Objective
Position the AI Audit as the logical next step to formalize the automation roadmap.

### The Audit Pitch Framework

**Step 1: Acknowledge the Potential**
> "It's clear there's significant opportunity here to streamline your operations with automation."

**Step 2: Position the Audit**
> "The next step would be to conduct a full AI Audit. This is a structured process where we map out your entire workflow, identify every bottleneck, and build you an official automation roadmap."

**Step 3: Explain What's Included**
> "In the audit, we'll:
> - Document your complete customer journey
> - Analyze your tech stack and integrations
> - Identify the highest-ROI automation opportunities
> - Create a prioritized implementation roadmap
> - Show you exactly what systems we'd build and how they'd work together"

**Step 4: Set the Value Frame**
> "The audit typically takes 1-2 weeks, and at the end, you'll have a complete blueprint for transforming your operationsâ€”whether you work with us or not. You'll own the roadmap."

**Step 5: Address Objections Preemptively**
> "Some clients ask if there's a cost for the audit. We do charge for it because of the depth of analysis involved, but it's designed to pay for itself many times over once the systems are implemented."

**Alternative (Free Audit Positioning):**
> "We include the audit at no charge as part of our onboarding process. It ensures we build exactly what you need, nothing more, nothing less."

## Phase 7: Next Steps - Book the Roadmap Call (2 minutes)

### Objective
Secure commitment to proceed with the AI Audit and book the next call.

### The Ask

> "So, [Name], based on everything we've discussed, does it make sense to move forward with the AI Audit and build out your automation roadmap?"

### Handle Response

**If YES:**
> "Perfect. The next step is to schedule a Roadmap Call where we'll go deeper into your workflows and begin the audit process. I'm looking at [Date/Time] or [Date/Time]. Which works better for you?"

**If MAYBE / Need to Think:**
> "Absolutely, I understand. What specific questions or concerns do you have that would help you make the decision?"

[Address concerns, then re-ask]

**If NO / Not Ready:**
> "No problem at all. Out of curiosity, what's holding you back?"

[Listen, address, and determine if nurture opportunity or disqualify]

### Booking the Call

- [ ] Send calendar invite immediately
- [ ] Confirm they received it
- [ ] Set expectations for next call (45-60 min, more detailed)
- [ ] Mention any pre-work (access, forms, etc.)

### Close Strong

> "Thanks so much for your time today, [Name]. I'm excited about the potential here. You'll receive a calendar invite for our Roadmap Call, and I'll send over a quick email recap of what we discussed. Looking forward to diving deeper."

## Red Flags During Call (Consider Disqualifying)

- [ ] No clear pain points even with probing
- [ ] Resistant to change or defensive
- [ ] Unrealistic expectations (wants everything for $500)
- [ ] Fishing for free consulting
- [ ] Not the real decision-maker and can't get access
- [ ] No urgency and no growth plans
- [ ] Already working with another automation agency

**Action:** Politely disengage and archive lead
