## SLIDE 6: Q1 - Problem Discovery

**QUESTION:**
"What is the biggest problem in your business right now?"

**PURPOSE:**
- Qualify the lead (do they have a real problem?)
- Understand their world (not yours)
- Build rapport through curiosity

**WHAT YOU'RE LEARNING:**
- Is there a problem we can solve?
- Do they recognize it?
- How do they describe it? (Use their words later!)

**COMMON MISTAKE:**
âŒ They tell you their problem â†’ You immediately pitch

**CORRECT APPROACH:**
âœ… They tell you their problem â†’ You validate, dig deeper, move to Q2

**Speaker Notes**: Your only job at Q1 is to LISTEN. Resist the urge to pitch. You don't know enough yet. Just listen, validate, and move to Q2.

## SLIDE 7: Q2 - Cost Quantification

**QUESTION:**
"How much is this problem costing the business per month?"

**PURPOSE:**
- Quantify the pain in dollars
- Establish your pricing ceiling
- Make the problem real (not abstract)

**WHAT YOU'RE LEARNING:**
- What's the budget? (You'll price at 30-50% of this number)
- How urgent is this? (Higher cost = more urgent)
- Do they understand the real cost? (If not, help them calculate)

**PRICING MATH:**
If Q2 = $20K/month problem â†’ You charge $6K-10K/month
Why? Because they net $10K-14K/month in savings = easy ROI

**Speaker Notes**: This is THE most important question for pricing. If they say "$50K/month problem" you can charge $15K-25K. If they say "$500/month problem" you're looking at $150-250. The problem cost dictates your price.

## SLIDE 8: Q2 - Handling "I Don't Know"

## SLIDE 9: Q3 - Timeline Assessment

**QUESTION:**

**WHAT YOU'RE LEARNING:**
- Is this new (low urgency) or old (high urgency)?
- Have they tried to fix it? What failed?

**Speaker Notes**: The longer they've had the problem, the more desperate they are for a solution. Use Q3 to amplify the cost over time.

## SLIDE 10: Q4 - Consequence Amplification

**QUESTION:**
"If you do nothing, what does the next 6 to 12 months look like?"

**Speaker Notes**: This is where you create urgency without being pushy. You're not creating fake scarcityâ€”you're helping them see the real consequence of inaction using their own logic.
