## Core Frameworks

### SHORT-FORM SCRIPT FRAMEWORK

**Hook (0-3 seconds)** — CRITICAL
Viewer decides to stay or scroll in first 3 seconds.

Hook Types:
- **Pattern Interrupt**: "Stop. If you're a business owner doing [X], this will save you 10 hours a week."
- **Bold Statement**: "AI isn't replacing jobs—it's replacing business owners who don't adapt."
- **Question Hook**: "Why do 80% of businesses fail to implement automation?"
- **Curiosity Gap**: "The #1 mistake I see founders make with AI..."
- **Controversy**: "Everyone's talking about AI. Almost everyone's wrong."

**Body (4-45 seconds)**
- One clear idea per video
- 3-5 key points maximum
- Each point: 1-2 sentences
- Pattern interrupt every 8-10 seconds (visual change, tone shift, text overlay)

**CTA (Final 5-15 seconds)**
- Single clear action
- Connect CTA to value delivered
- Examples:
  - "Follow for more systems that actually work"
  - "DM 'AUDIT' for your free AI efficiency scan"
  - "Link in bio for the full framework"

### LONG-FORM SCRIPT FRAMEWORK

**Cold Open (0-30 seconds)**
Hook with the transformation or outcome first.
"By the end of this video, you'll have a complete framework for [X]..."

**Introduction (30 sec - 1 min)**
- Who this is for
- What problem it solves
- Preview of what's covered
- Credibility statement (brief)

**Body (Main Content)**
Structure Options:
- **Numbered List**: "5 ways to automate your business"
- **Step-by-Step**: "The exact process we use with clients"
- **Framework**: "The CLEAR system for AI implementation"
- **Case Study**: "How [Client] achieved [Result]"

For each section:
- Clear transition
- One main point
- Supporting example or data
- B-roll direction in brackets
- Pattern interrupt marker every 2-3 minutes

**Summary/Recap**
- Restate key points (30 seconds)
- Reinforce transformation

**CTA (Final 60-90 seconds)**
- What to do next
- What they'll get
- How to take action

## Visual & B-Roll Direction

### How to Write B-Roll Notes

In brackets, provide:
- What to show (screen recording, stock footage, graphic)
- Duration suggestion
- Text overlay content
- Transition type

**Examples:**
```
[B-ROLL: Screen recording of Notion dashboard, 5 sec]
[B-ROLL: Stock footage - person overwhelmed at desk, 3 sec]
[TEXT OVERLAY: "40% reduction in admin time"]
[CUT TO: Close-up shot, higher energy]
[GRAPHIC: Step 1-2-3 animation]
```

### Pattern Interrupt Markers

Mark with [PATTERN INTERRUPT] where editor should add:
- Camera angle change
- Text animation
- Sound effect
- B-roll cut
- Zoom in/out
- Music beat drop

## Brand Voice for Video

### Delivery Style

**On Camera**:
- Confident but not arrogant
- Direct eye contact (camera lens)
- Conversational pace
- Strategic pauses for emphasis
- Energy matches content importance

**Script Language**:
- Short sentences for punch
- One idea per sentence
- Active voice always
- No filler words ("um," "like," "you know")
- Data when it strengthens point

### AriseGroup.ai Video Voice

**Do**:
- Lead with insights, not introductions
- Use concrete numbers and outcomes
- Share frameworks and systems
- Reference real client scenarios (anonymized)
- End with clear next step

**Don't**:
- Over-promise ("transform your life!")
- Use clickbait that doesn't deliver
- Ramble without structure
- Sound like a generic guru
- Forget the CTA

## Retention Mechanics

### Hook Engineering

**First 3 seconds must**:
- Create curiosity gap
- Challenge assumption
- Promise specific outcome
- Use unexpected visual

### Loop Technique

Open a loop early, close it later:
- "I'll share the exact framework in a minute, but first..."
- "The third point is the one that changed everything..."
- "Most people get this wrong—I'll show you why..."

### Pattern Interrupt Cadence

- Short-form: Every 8-10 seconds
- Long-form: Every 2-3 minutes
- Types: Visual cut, tone shift, question, story, data point

### Engagement Triggers

- Direct questions: "Here's the question..."
- Inclusive language: "If you're like most founders..."
- Specific callouts: "This is especially for [audience]..."
- Prediction: "I bet you've experienced this..."
